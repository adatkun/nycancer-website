Radionuclide Therapies

Targeted radiopharmaceutical treatments designed to deliver therapeutic radiation to tumors while helping to spare healthy tissues.

What are Radionuclide Therapies?

Radionuclide therapies utilize radiopharmaceutical drugs that target specific types of tumors. Releasing energy, these drugs can bring radiation to tumors in targeted doses and therefore sparing normal and healthy tissues.

A radiopharmaceutical consists of a radioisotope ion bonded to an organic molecule, which in turn conveys the radioisotope to the targeted organ, tissue, or cell. The choice of the radioisotope depends on the affinity or binding power to target structures of the tumor such as antigens and receptors.

The radiation from the radioactive nuclide damages the DNA of the cancer cells, killing them in the process and shrinking the tumors. Radiopharmaceuticals not only effectively eliminate malignant tumor cells but also help spare healthy tissues and organs.

How It Works

Radionuclide therapy uses carefully selected radioisotopes to deliver therapeutic radiation to targeted tissues.

1

Targeting

A radioisotope is bonded to a carrier molecule that can travel to a specific organ, tissue, or tumor target.

2

Delivery

The radiopharmaceutical is typically administered intravenously, allowing it to circulate and bind where intended.

3

Therapeutic Effect

The ionizing radiation emitted by the radionuclide determines the biological activity of the radiopharmaceutical. Tumor therapy typically uses alpha or beta emitters, which deliver radiation into nearby tissues.

4

Follow-Up

Your care team will review response and symptoms and guide next steps based on your diagnosis and treatment plan.

Radionuclide Therapies

Treatments may be used for palliation or therapy depending on diagnosis and care plan.

Palliatives

Xofigo

Xofigo is a radioactive agent used for the treatment of prostate cancer with metastatic bone disease. It is administered intravenously as a slow injection over a minute. Injections are performed monthly over the course of 6 months.

Quadramet

Quadramet is an injectable radiopharmaceutical for treating bone pain associated with various cancer types. Given as a single injection. It can be repeated if your physician determines it may benefit symptom relief.

Therapeutic

Zevalin

Zevalin is a treatment for Follicular Lymphoma that combines two effective therapies in a single course of treatment: immunotherapy and radiotherapy. The entire treatment regimen can be completed in 7-9 days. It is administered intravenously.

Care Planning

Eligibility, preparation, and aftercare are individualized. Your provider will review your medical history and treatment goals to determine the most appropriate approach.
