Joint Injections

Precise, image-guided steroid injections for lasting relief from joint pain

Image-Guided Precision

Relief for Joint Pain

Joint injections deliver steroids directly into the joint space to effectively treat arthritis, tendonitis, and bursitis. By placing medication precisely where it's needed, these injections can provide significant relief from pain and inflammation.

Using x-ray guidance, your interventional radiologist ensures the steroid mixture reaches the exact location for maximum therapeutic benefit. This precision approach helps optimize results while minimizing discomfort.

It can take up to 10 days for you to feel the full effects of the steroid. Depending on your condition and how your body responds, the relief can last anywhere from several weeks to many months.

During Your Procedure

A quick, precise process for targeted relief

1

Joint Localization

Your doctor uses x-ray guidance to precisely identify and visualize the joint that will be treated.

2

Site Preparation

The skin over the joint is thoroughly cleaned to ensure a sterile environment for the injection.

3

Needle Placement

A thin needle is carefully advanced into the joint space under continuous x-ray guidance for precise positioning.

4

Steroid Injection

Once the needle tip is confirmed to be in the joint space, the steroid mixture is injected to deliver targeted relief.

What to Expect

Up to 10 Days For full effects to be felt
Weeks to Months Duration of relief varies by patient

After Your Procedure

  • You'll be able to go home shortly after the injection
  • Some discomfort at the site is normal and typically improves within 24-48 hours
  • Over-the-counter pain medication can help if needed
  • Most patients don't require any pain medication

Caring for Your Injection Site

  • Do not get the area wet for 24 hours
  • After 24 hours, you may shower but continue avoiding getting the site wet
  • No baths or swimming for 3 days

When to Call Your Doctor

Contact us if you experience:

Fever of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher
Chills or unexplained shaking
