IR Preprocedural Instructions

Important information to help you prepare for your interventional radiology procedure

Questions about your procedure? (516) 407-6317 irscheduling@nyimaging.com Fax: (516) 407-6338

COVID-19 Vaccination & Testing

Either a valid COVID-19 vaccination card or a negative COVID-19 test must be obtained within five (5) days prior to your scheduled procedure.

Accepted Tests

  • PCR test
  • Antigen test

Testing can be done at our office.

Outside Test Results

If you have an outside test, results must be faxed and emailed to our office at least 24 hours prior to your scheduled procedure.

Feeling sick? If you have a fever, cold, sore throat, or flu symptoms before your procedure, call your doctor in Interventional Radiology immediately.

Ask About Your Medications

You may need to stop taking some of your medications before your procedure. Talk with your healthcare provider about which medications are safe for you to stop taking. Whether they recommend you stop taking the medication depends on the type of procedure you're having and the reason you're taking blood thinners.

Preparation Timeline

Follow these important steps before your procedure

The Day Before Your Procedure

Do not eat anything after midnight the night before your procedure. This includes hard candy and gum.

The Day of Your Procedure

No Food Do not eat anything the day of your procedure.
Limited Water Only You can drink a total of 8 ounces of water between midnight and 2 hours before your scheduled arrival time. Do not drink anything else.
Nothing 2 Hours Before Do not drink anything starting 2 hours before your scheduled arrival time. This includes water.
Regular Medications Please take your regular medications unless otherwise instructed by your healthcare provider.

Arrange for Someone to Take You Home

You must have a responsible care partner take you home after your procedure. A responsible care partner is someone who can help you get home safely and report concerns to your healthcare providers, if needed.

Important

Make sure to plan this before the day of your procedure.

Don't have a care partner?

You may use an agency that can send someone to go home with you. There's usually a charge for this service, and you'll need to provide transportation. It's OK to use a taxi or car service, but you must still have a responsible care partner with you.
