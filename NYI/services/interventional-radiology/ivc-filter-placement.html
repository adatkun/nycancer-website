IVC Filter Placement

Protecting your lungs from dangerous blood clots with a minimally invasive solution

Clot Protection

What is an IVC Filter?

The inferior vena cava (IVC) is the largest vein in your body, carrying blood from your legs back to your heart and lungs. When blood clots form in your legs, they can potentially travel through this vein to your lungs, creating serious breathing problems—a condition known as pulmonary embolism.

While blood clots are typically treated with blood thinners, some patients cannot safely receive this treatment. In these cases, an IVC filter offers an effective alternative. This small, specialized device is placed inside your IVC to catch and prevent clots from traveling to your lungs.

Your interventional radiologist will place the IVC filter through a vein in your neck or groin using fluoroscopy (video x-rays) for precise guidance. The procedure takes approximately 30 minutes, though you should expect to be in the procedure room for about an hour total.

Before Your Procedure

Preparing for your IVC filter placement

IV Line Placement

An IV line will be placed in your arm to deliver fluids and medications throughout your procedure.

Sedation for Comfort

You may receive medication through your IV that will help you feel relaxed and drowsy, controlling any pain and anxiety.

During Your Procedure

What happens during IVC filter placement

1

Site Preparation

The area where the vein will be accessed (either at the base of your neck or in your groin) will be thoroughly cleaned and numbed with local anesthetic (lidocaine/novocaine).

2

Vein Access

Your interventional radiologist will carefully access a vein at the base of your neck or in your groin, depending on what's best for your specific case.

3

Filter Placement

Using fluoroscopy (video x-rays) for real-time guidance, the IVC filter is precisely positioned within your inferior vena cava to effectively catch any blood clots.

4

Site Closure

The access site will be covered with a sterile bandage or sealed with surgical glue called Dermabond® for quick, comfortable healing.

After Your Procedure

Your recovery and follow-up care

Recovery Observation

  • You'll stay in our recovery area for approximately 1 hour
  • Our team will monitor you to ensure you're fully awake and feeling well
  • Some discomfort at the access site is normal and should improve within 24-48 hours
  • Over-the-counter pain medication may help if needed, though most patients don't require any

Caring for Your Access Site

  • Do not get the area wet for the first 24 hours
  • After 24 hours, you may shower but continue to avoid getting the site wet
  • No baths or swimming for at least 5 days

Dermabond Note: Don't pick or scratch the surgical glue. It will naturally come off on its own as your site heals.

Your Follow-Up Appointment

You'll have a follow-up appointment 4 to 6 weeks after your IVC filter placement. During this visit, your doctor will evaluate when your filter can be safely removed.

IVC filters are often temporary devices. Once the risk of blood clots has passed or you can safely take blood thinners, the filter may be removed through a similar minimally invasive procedure.

When to Call Your Doctor

Contact your healthcare provider immediately if you experience:

Fever Temperature of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher
Chills Unexplained shaking or feeling cold
