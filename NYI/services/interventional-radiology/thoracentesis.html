Chest Fluid Drainage

What is Thoracentesis?

Thoracentesis, also known as a chest fluid tap, is a minimally invasive procedure that removes excess fluid that has built up in your chest around your lungs. This fluid accumulation, called a pleural effusion, can cause significant discomfort, pain, and difficulty breathing.

By draining this fluid, thoracentesis provides quick relief from these symptoms and helps you breathe more easily. The removed fluid is sometimes sent to a laboratory for analysis, which can help your doctors better understand what's causing the fluid buildup.

Benefits of Thoracentesis:

  • Relieves chest pain and pressure
  • Improves breathing by allowing lungs to expand fully
  • Allows fluid analysis for diagnostic purposes

During Your Procedure

A quick, comfortable process for breathing relief

1

Ultrasound Localization

Your interventional radiologist uses ultrasound to identify the area in your chest with the most fluid accumulation.

2

Site Preparation

The selected area is thoroughly cleaned and numbed with local anesthetic (lidocaine/novocaine) for your comfort.

3

Catheter Insertion

A small catheter (plastic tube) is carefully inserted into the fluid collection around your lungs.

4

Drainage

The excess fluid is gently drained from your chest. The time required depends on the amount of fluid present.

5

Completion

Once enough fluid has been removed, the catheter is withdrawn and the puncture site is covered with skin glue (Dermabond®) or a small bandage.

After Your Procedure

Recovery and site care instructions

Recovery

  • You'll rest in our recovery area for a short time after the procedure
  • Tell your doctor or nurse if you feel dizzy or lightheaded
  • Some pain or discomfort is normal
  • Over-the-counter medications like Tylenol® or Advil® can help if needed

Caring for Your Puncture Site

  • Do not get the area wet for 24 hours
  • After 24 hours, you may shower but continue avoiding getting the site wet
  • No baths or swimming for at least 3 days

When to Call Your Doctor

Contact your healthcare provider immediately if you experience:

New or Worsening Shortness of Breath Any difficulty breathing that develops or gets worse after your procedure
