Nephrostomy Tube Placement

Restoring kidney drainage when urinary blockages occur

Kidney Drainage

What is a Nephrostomy Tube?

A nephrostomy tube is a small, flexible plastic tube that drains urine directly from your kidney. When there's a blockage in your urinary system preventing normal urine flow, this tube provides an alternate path for drainage, protecting your kidney from damage.

Blockages in the urinary system can be caused by a variety of conditions, including kidney stones, scarring from previous procedures or infections, and cancer. By bypassing the blockage, the nephrostomy tube allows your kidney to function properly while the underlying condition is treated.

Before Your Procedure

Preparing for your nephrostomy tube placement

IV Line Placement

An IV line will be placed in your arm to deliver fluids and medications during the procedure.

Sedation for Comfort

You may receive medication through your IV that will help you feel relaxed and drowsy, controlling any pain and anxiety.

During Your Procedure

What happens during nephrostomy tube placement

1

Site Preparation

The area where the kidney will be accessed (through your back or side) is thoroughly cleaned and numbed with local anesthetic (lidocaine/novocaine).

2

Ultrasound Guidance

Your doctor uses ultrasound imaging to visualize your kidney and guide a small needle precisely into the kidney's drainage system.

3

Wire Placement

Once the needle is in the correct position, a thin guidewire is inserted through the needle to maintain access to the kidney.

4

Tube Insertion

The needle is removed and the flexible plastic nephrostomy tube is advanced over the wire into your kidney, where it will drain urine into an external collection bag.

After Your Procedure

Recovery and caring for your nephrostomy tube

Recovery Observation

  • You'll stay in our recovery area for 3-4 hours
  • Our team monitors you to ensure you're fully awake and feeling well
  • Some discomfort at the access site is normal and typically improves within 24-48 hours
  • Over-the-counter pain medication can help if needed; most patients don't require any

Caring for Your Access Site

  • Do not get the area wet for 24 hours
  • After 24 hours, you may shower but continue avoiding getting the site wet
  • No baths or swimming for the entire time you have the nephrostomy tube

What's Normal After Your Procedure

It's normal for your urine to appear pink or slightly bloody following the procedure. This should clear up within 2-3 days as your body heals. However, if your urine becomes progressively bloodier instead of improving, contact your doctor.

When to Call Your Doctor

Contact your healthcare provider immediately if you experience:

Fever Temperature of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher
Chills Unexplained shaking or feeling cold
Worsening Blood in Urine Urine gets progressively bloodier instead of clearing
Bleeding Around Tube Active bleeding at the tube insertion site
