Uterine Fibroid Embolization (UFE)

A minimally invasive alternative to surgery for treating uterine fibroids

Fibroid Treatment

What is Uterine Fibroid Embolization?

Uterine Fibroid Embolization (UFE) is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat fibroids of the uterus. Fibroids are non-cancerous growths that can cause heavy menstrual bleeding, pain, and pressure on the bladder or bowel.

UFE works by blocking blood flow to the fibroids, causing them to shrink and relieving associated symptoms. During the procedure, your interventional radiologist threads a small catheter (flexible tube) into your uterine artery and injects tiny particles (embolics) that block blood flow to the fibroids.

Before Your Procedure

Several tests ensure UFE is the right treatment for you

Pap Smear

Performed at your gynecologist's office

Endometrial Biopsy

Tissue sample to rule out other conditions

MRI Scan

Detailed imaging of your uterus and fibroids

These tests ensure there is nothing else causing your symptoms besides your fibroids.

On the Day of Your Procedure

IV Line Placement

An IV will be placed in your arm for fluids and medications.

Sedation for Comfort

Medication will help you feel relaxed and drowsy, controlling pain and anxiety.

Urinary Catheter

May be placed at the beginning of the procedure if needed.

During Your Procedure

What happens during Uterine Fibroid Embolization

1

Access Point Selection

Using ultrasound guidance, your interventional radiologist selects an artery in your groin or wrist as the access point.

2

Site Preparation

The area is thoroughly cleaned and numbed with local anesthetic (lidocaine/novocaine).

3

Catheter Navigation

Using fluoroscopy (video x-ray), the interventional radiologist guides the catheter to both the left and right uterine arteries.

4

Position Confirmation

Contrast dye is used to confirm the catheter is positioned correctly in each uterine artery.

5

Embolization

Tiny particles are injected to block blood flow to the fibroids until adequate reduction is achieved on both sides.

6

Completion

The catheter is removed and the access site is covered with a sterile dressing.

After Your Procedure

Recovery begins in our care

Recovery Observation

  • You'll stay in our recovery area for 2-4 hours
  • Our team monitors you to ensure you're fully awake and feeling well

Groin Access: If we accessed through your groin, you'll need to lie down and keep that leg straight during recovery.

Caring for Your Access Site

  • Do not get the area wet for 24 hours
  • After 24 hours, you may shower but avoid getting the site wet
  • No baths or swimming for at least 7 days

Recovery at Home

Understanding post-embolization syndrome

Post-Embolization Syndrome

It's common to experience some symptoms after UFE as your body responds to the treatment. These typically improve within 3-5 days:

Nausea and/or vomiting Can often be managed with medication
Pelvic pain or cramping Most common side effect as blood supply is reduced
Low-grade fever May last up to 1 week
Lethargy and fatigue Typically resolves within 1 week

Contact your doctor if these symptoms persist beyond 10 days.

Activity Restrictions

For the first 5 days after your procedure:

No Heavy Lifting Don't lift anything heavier than 10 pounds
No Rigorous Exercise Avoid strenuous physical activity
Light Activities OK Resume usual light activities after 24 hours

When to Call Your Doctor

Contact your healthcare provider immediately if you experience:

Fever Temperature of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher
Uncontrolled Symptoms Pain, nausea, or vomiting not relieved by medication
Access Site Changes Redness, swelling, or bleeding around the site
Any Concerning Symptoms Anything that worries you about your recovery
