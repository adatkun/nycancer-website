Transthoracic Echocardiogram (TTE)

A noninvasive ultrasound exam that creates moving images of your heart through the chest wall to help evaluate overall heart health.

What is a Transthoracic Echocardiogram (TTE)?

A Transthoracic Echocardiogram (TTE) is a noninvasive ultrasound exam that uses high-frequency soundwaves to create moving images of your heart through the chest wall.

No Radiation

There is no radiation when getting a TTE.

A TTE helps your doctor evaluate:

  • Heart size
  • The amount of blood being pumped out of your heart
  • Valve function
  • Injury to the heart muscle

What to Expect During Your TTE

This exam is comfortable and requires no special preparation.

1

Before Your Exam

No special preparation is required before your TTE.

2

During the Exam

The exam may take up to 1 hour. You will be attached to electrodes to monitor your heart rate and electrical activity.

3

Positioning

You will be lying down for most of the exam and positioned on your side when possible to obtain optimal imaging.

4

After the Exam

You can typically return to normal activities immediately unless your provider gives you different instructions.

Who reads my TTE?

Unlike other radiology exams, your TTE is read by a Cardiologist as opposed to a Radiologist.
