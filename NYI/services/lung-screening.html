Lung Cancer Screening

Low-dose CT (LDCT) lung cancer screening to help detect lung cancer early in eligible patients, with fast scheduling and same-day radiology reads.

What is Lung Cancer Screening?

Lung cancer screening uses a low-dose CT scan (LDCT) to look for early signs of lung cancer in patients who meet specific eligibility guidelines. LDCT uses much less radiation than a standard CT scan and does not require needles.

Screening is designed for people at higher risk due to cigarette smoking history. Finding lung cancer early can improve treatment options and outcomes.

If you are not sure whether you qualify, you can use the eligibility form on this page to help guide next steps.

Screening Guidelines

Who May Be Eligible?

Lung cancer screening is generally recommended for people who meet age and cigarette smoking history requirements. Coverage rules can vary by insurance.

Age Range

You must be at least age 50-80 depending on insurance coverage.

Cigarettes Only

This screening is for cigarette smoking history, not cigars or vaping.

Quick Exam

The scan is typically completed in minutes and does not require contrast.

Board-Certified Reads

Images are reviewed by board-certified radiologists and sent to your provider.

Important Notes

Lung cancer screening is intended for patients without symptoms of lung cancer. If you have new or concerning symptoms, please contact your healthcare provider promptly.

Knowing what to expect can help you feel comfortable and prepared for your screening exam.

Preparing for Your Lung Screening

Most patients do not need special preparation for a low-dose CT lung screening.

What to Wear

  • Wear comfortable clothing
  • Avoid metal buttons or heavy jewelry if possible
  • A gown can be provided if needed

What to Bring

  • Photo ID and insurance card
  • Referring provider information (if applicable)
  • A list of your current medications

Pregnancy Notice

Please inform our staff if there is any chance you may be pregnant before your exam.
