What is MRI?

MRI, or Magnetic Resonance Imaging, is a non-invasive, needle-free, and safe imaging technique that uses magnetic fields and radio frequencies to generate detailed anatomical images of the organs and tissues within your body.

Unlike X-rays or CT scans, MRI doesn't use ionizing radiation, making it an excellent choice for detailed soft tissue imaging and repeated examinations when needed.

Our advanced MRI technology provides your physicians with the high-quality diagnostic images they need to accurately assess your condition and develop the most effective treatment plan.

How MRI Works

You'll be asked to remove any metal objects and change into comfortable clothing. Our staff will review your medical history and answer any questions.

2

Positioning

You'll lie on a motorized bed that slides into the cylindrical scanner. The machine is open at both ends, and our technologist will ensure you're comfortable.

Preparing for Your MRI

Proper preparation ensures the best possible imaging results and a smooth examination experience.

What to Wear

  • Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing
  • Avoid clothing with metal zippers or clasps
  • Leave jewelry and accessories at home
  • Avoid underwire bras
  • A gown will be provided if needed

IV Contrast Preparation

  • A simple kidney function blood test is required
  • Test can be done in-office in under 5 minutes
  • No other preparation is required
  • You may eat and drink normally

Important: Implants & MRI Safety

Please notify your physician and our technologist of any implants within your body before entering the MRI machine. Certain implants cannot safely enter the MRI environment. Also, please inform us if there is any chance you may be pregnant.
