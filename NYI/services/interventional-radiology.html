What Is Interventional Radiology?

Interventional radiology is a cutting-edge field of medicine that uses minimally invasive approaches to treat many conditions which otherwise would require more invasive surgery.

Interventional Radiologists perform these procedures using image guidance such as X-ray, CT, ultrasound, and MRI to precisely navigate to the treatment area.

Many procedures are performed with small needle or catheter access, which often means fewer complications and a faster return to normal activities.

How Interventional Radiology Works

Patients who have interventional radiological procedures do not need a large incision and often do not require general anesthesia.

In many cases, these procedures are less risky, and recovery times are shorter than with traditional surgery.

Most patients are able to go home within a few hours of their procedure.

Interventional Radiology Treatments

Image-guided procedures that support diagnosis, treatment, and symptom relief.

Diagnosis & Access

We help to diagnose cancers and other diseases with image-guided needle biopsies. We place ports and catheters in order to allow infusion of chemotherapy and other medications.

Cancer Treatments

We treat many types of cancer, primarily through arterial embolization and thermal ablation. For arterial embolization, we inject particles directly into the blood vessels that supply a tumor in order to cut off its blood supply.

Thermal Ablation

In thermal ablation, we use imaging to guide a needle to a target tumor and then destroy it with heat.

Additional Procedures

We also perform a variety of non-cancer-related procedures such as kyphoplasty and joint injections for pain, and uterine artery embolization for fibroids.
