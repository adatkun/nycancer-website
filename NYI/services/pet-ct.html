What is a PET/CT Scan?

A PET/CT scan combines two powerful imaging technologies to determine the exact location of abnormalities in the body and detect diseases such as cancer with exceptional accuracy.

Positron emission tomography (PET) uses controlled amounts of a radioactive substance called a radiotracer to measure cell activity in organs and tissues. Computed tomography (CT) uses special X-ray equipment to produce detailed anatomical images.

By combining metabolic function and anatomical information, PET/CT provides a more detailed picture of cancerous tissues than either test alone. The images are captured in a single scan, delivering a high level of diagnostic accuracy.

Molecular Imaging

How PET/CT Works

PET/CT scanning uses radiotracersâ€”molecules linked to small amounts of radioactive material detectable on the PET scan. The most commonly used radiotracer is F-18 fluorodeoxyglucose (FDG), a molecule similar to glucose.

Cancer cells absorb glucose at a higher rate than normal cells, causing the radiotracer to accumulate in tumors and areas of inflammation. This activity is detected by the PET/CT scanner, producing detailed images of both structure and function.

The radiotracer is injected through an IV line or port, then given time to accumulate in the target areas before scanning begins.

Common Uses for PET/CT

PET/CT is primarily used for cancer detection, staging, and monitoring, with applications across many cancer types.

Lung Cancer

Detection, staging, and treatment response monitoring for lung malignancies.

Breast Cancer

Evaluates spread of disease and monitors response to therapy.

Lymphoma

Staging and assessing treatment effectiveness for lymphatic cancers.

Colorectal Cancer

Detects active malignant lesions and monitors treatment response.

Melanoma

Determines extent of disease spread for skin cancer staging.

Multiple Myeloma

Detects bone lesions and monitors disease progression.

What to Expect

PET/CT scanning is a non-invasive outpatient procedure. Plan to be at our facility for approximately 2-3 hours.

1

Check-In & Prep

Your blood sugar level is checked to ensure it's within scan parameters. An IV line is placed and the radiotracer is injected.

2

Uptake Period

Rest quietly on a recliner for approximately one hour while the radiotracer accumulates. Avoid movement and talking during this time.

3

The Scan

Empty your bladder, then lie on the scanning bed. CT scan takes less than 2 minutes; PET scan takes approximately 25 minutes.

4

After the Exam

The IV is removed. Drink plenty of fluids to flush the radiotracer from your system through your kidneys.

Important Post-Scan Information

We advise patients to stay away from children and pregnant women for 6 to 24 hours following injection. We are extremely cautious in this regard; there are NO definite risks from this level of radiation.

Preparing for Your PET/CT Scan

Proper preparation is essential for accurate PET/CT results. Please follow these guidelines carefully.

24 Hours Before

  • No caffeine
  • No exercise or strenuous physical activity
  • Follow the low-carb diet plan (see below)

6 Hours Before

  • Do NOT eat (including tube feeding)
  • No gum, cough drops, or mints
  • No smoking
  • Drink ONLY plain water
  • Take prescribed non-diabetic medications

Day of Exam

  • Wear comfortable, warm clothing
  • No jewelry or metal buttons
  • No underwire bras or metal zippers
  • Bring prior PET/CT or CT discs from other facilities
  • Notify us if claustrophobic or pregnant

Diabetic Patients

  • Insulin-controlled: Have meal with insulin 4 hours prior
  • Morning appt: Don't take diabetic tablets; bring them to take after
  • Afternoon appt: Take tablets in morning, 6+ hours before scan

PET/CT Diet Plan

Follow this low-carbohydrate diet for 24 hours before your exam to ensure optimal image quality.

Foods to Eat

  • Protein: Beef, chicken, turkey, fish, pork, eggs, peanut butter
  • Dairy: Cottage cheese, cheese, light yogurt (artificial sweetener), butter
  • Vegetables: Green beans, broccoli, cauliflower, spinach, lettuce, celery
  • Condiments: Mayo, mustard, oil, vinegar, dill pickles, olives
  • Beverages: Water, diet soda, sugar-free drinks, milk

Foods to Avoid

  • Fruits & Starches: All fruits, potatoes, corn, carrots, beans, rice
  • Breads & Grains: All bread, pasta, crackers, rice cakes
  • Beverages: Beer, wine, liquor, juices
  • Snacks: Chips, pretzels, candy, gum, cough drops
  • Other: Syrups, jams, ketchup, sugary sauces

Menu Suggestions

Breakfast: Bacon/sausage and eggs, ham and cheese omelet, light yogurt
Lunch: Egg salad, chef salad (no tomato), ham & cheese wrapped in lettuce
Dinner: Cheeseburger (no bun), chicken with BBQ sauce, veggie soup with broth
Snack: Celery with peanut butter, cottage cheese
