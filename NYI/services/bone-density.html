Bone Density (DEXA) Scan

A quick, low-radiation, painless exam that measures bone strength and helps assess osteoporosis and fracture risk.

What is a Bone Density Scan?

A Bone Density scan (often called a DEXA scan) is an imaging test that measures bone density (strength). Results provide helpful details about your risk for osteoporosis (bone loss) and fractures (bone breaks).

In some cases, this exam may also measure body composition, such as body fat and muscle mass.

Medical experts consider Bone Density scans to be one of the most useful, easy, and inexpensive tests for helping to diagnose osteoporosis. The test is quick and painless.

Advanced Technology

How DEXA Works

During a Bone Density scan, a specialized imaging arm passes over the body and uses two different X-ray beams. These beams use very little radiation to keep the test safer and help distinguish bone from other tissues.

Bone Density scans measure the mineral content in specific areas such as the hip, spine, and/or wrist.

Your results are reviewed and interpreted by a radiologist or other physician trained in Bone Density interpretation, and a written report is sent to your healthcare provider.

Who Gets a Bone Density Scan?

Bone Density testing is often recommended when risk factors for bone loss or fracture are present.

Increased Age

Many individuals lose bone mass as they get older.

Family History

If one or more family members have had osteoporosis or more than one fracture, you could be at higher risk.

Previous Fracture Injuries

Breaking a bone, especially after age 50, may signify a greater risk because less dense bones break more easily.

Certain Medications

Some medications, such as steroid prednisone, cancer drugs, and post-transplant drugs, can weaken bones.

Overall Health

Some chronic conditions can increase fracture risk, including rheumatoid arthritis, lupus, diabetes, liver disease, and kidney disease.

Provider Recommendation

Your clinician may order a scan to establish a baseline or monitor bone health over time.

Helpful for Prevention

A Bone Density scan can identify bone loss early, when prevention and treatment strategies can be most effective.

What to Expect During the Bone Density Scan

Understanding the process can help you feel more comfortable and prepared for your exam.

1

Check-In

Our team will review your order and confirm the type of Bone Density scan you are scheduled to receive.

2

Positioning

You will lie on a special Bone Density X-ray table. The technologist will help position you correctly and may use positioning devices such as foam blocks to help hold the desired position.

3

During the Scan

The imaging arm passes over the body to measure bone mineral content in targeted areas such as the hip, spine, and/or wrist. The exam is quick and painless.

4

Results & Next Steps

Your images are interpreted by a radiologist or physician trained in Bone Density interpretation. Your healthcare provider receives a written report to review with you and discuss appropriate treatment options.

Preparing for Your Bone Density Scan

A few simple steps can help your exam go smoothly.

What to Wear

  • Wear comfortable clothing
  • Avoid clothing with metal zippers, hooks, or buttons
  • Remove belts, jewelry, or metal accessories before the scan
  • A gown may be provided if needed

Before You Arrive

  • Bring your prescription/order and insurance information
  • Tell us about prior fractures or bone-related diagnoses
  • Share a current medication list if available
  • If you may be pregnant, please notify our staff before the scan

Important

Please let our team know if there is any chance you may be pregnant. Also inform us of any recent imaging tests or procedures your provider wants us to be aware of.
