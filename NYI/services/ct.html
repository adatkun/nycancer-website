CT Scan Services

Advanced computed tomography imaging providing detailed 3D views of your body's internal structures for accurate diagnosis and treatment planning.

What is a CT Scan?

A CT scan (computed tomography), also known as a CAT scan, is a non-invasive diagnostic imaging procedure that combines computer technology with X-rays to generate accurate and detailed images of body structures including organs, blood vessels, tissues, and bones.

Widely regarded as one of the most important medical developments of the past century, CT scanning is a valuable diagnostic tool that allows specialists to more easily and accurately diagnose diseases.

Unlike standard X-rays which generate two-dimensional views, CT scans create detailed 360-degree images by rotating an X-ray machine around your body, taking multiple thin horizontal slices that a computer assembles into a three-dimensional model.

How CT Scans Work

During a CT scan, you lie on a motorized table that moves through a large machine with a circular opening. As the table advances, an X-ray source rotates around the opening, capturing multiple images from different angles.

Each rotation yields different images of internal organs and tissues. All data from the X-ray source is digitally transmitted to a computer that reconstructs individual shots into detailed cross-sectional images.

In some cases, a contrast agent may be administered intravenously, orally, or both to help visualize organs, blood vessels, and structures that are otherwise difficult to see clearly.

What to Expect During Your CT Scan

CT scans are painless procedures that typically take 15-20 minutes including preparation and contrast administration.

1

Check-In

You'll complete any necessary paperwork and questionnaires. If contrast is required, preparation will begin based on your specific exam.

2

Positioning

You'll lie on a motorized table and may be positioned with straps and pillows to help you stay still during the scan.

3

During the Scan

The table slowly moves through the scanner. You may hear buzzing and whirring sounds but won't feel anything. Stay still and hold your breath briefly when instructed.

4

Results

Images are processed and transmitted to a radiologist for review. A report will be generated and sent to your ordering physician.

Stay Connected

During your scan, the technologist will be in an adjacent control room but can see you and communicate with you through an intercom at all times.

Preparing for Your CT Scan

Preparation varies depending on your specific exam. Please follow the instructions provided by your physician.

General Guidelines

  • Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing
  • Avoid clothing with metal zippers or clasps
  • Leave jewelry and accessories at home
  • Avoid underwire bras
  • Notify your physician if you may be pregnant

IV Contrast Patients

  • Fast for 3 hours prior (clear liquids OK)
  • Creatinine blood test required within 30 days
  • Complete questionnaire and consent form
  • Inform us of any prior contrast allergies

Oral Contrast Patients

  • Fast for 3 hours prior (clear liquids OK)
  • Mix 30mL Gastroview into 500mL water bottle
  • Begin drinking 1.5 hours before appointment
  • Bring bottle to finish in waiting room

Diabetic Patients

  • Recent BUN/Creatinine required within 4 weeks
  • Discuss medications with your physician
  • Some medications held 48 hours after IV contrast
  • Includes: Metformin, Glucophage, Janumet, others

Important: Contrast Allergies

Please inform your physician if you have any allergy to contrast agents or have had an allergic reaction in the past. This is critical for your safety during the exam.

Common Uses for CT Scans

CT scanning is a versatile diagnostic tool used to evaluate a wide range of conditions throughout the body.

Pain Evaluation

Commonly used in the workup of abdominal or chest pain to identify underlying causes.

Cancer Detection

Visualizes tumors and assesses their size, location, and potential involvement with other tissues.

Injury Assessment

Detects fractures, injuries, blood clots, hemorrhages, and other trauma-related issues.

Lung Screening

Low-dose CT recommended for adults at high-risk of lung cancer due to smoking history.

Infection Detection

Identifies infections and inflammatory conditions throughout the body.

Stroke Assessment

Evaluates bleeding, blood clots, and other signs of stroke in the brain.
