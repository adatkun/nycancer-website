Benefits Counseling

Expert guidance for navigating insurance and financial assistance during cancer treatment.

At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we understand that managing the financial aspects of cancer treatment can be overwhelming. Our dedicated benefits counselors are here to provide personalized support and guidance to help you navigate insurance coverage, identify financial assistance programs, and minimize financial toxicity during your treatment journey.

The Role of Our Benefits Counselors

The role of the oncology benefit counselor is to guide patients who may potentially face financial toxicity when going through the hardship of chemotherapy. Our highly trained counselors will guide patients through the insurance process and obtain any available assistance that may meet their needs.

Our benefit counselors will meet one-on-one or with patient's families to educate all parties on their current insurance and the type of coverage it entails. They will review the patient's deductible, out-of-pocket, co-insurance, co-pays, and estimated cost of treatment.

Nutritional Counseling Support

In addition to benefits counseling, we at New York Cancer and Blood Specialists believe in a multidisciplinary, and holistic approach to cancer treatment. We strongly believe in exercise and proper nutritional counseling during your care with us.

For your benefit, we offer on-site nutritional counseling while you are on treatment. We will have our registered dietitian speak with you at various points during your therapy to make sure we are doing all that we can to help you.

Nutrition During and After Cancer Treatment

The goal of nutrition therapy during and after your treatment is to help you get the nutrients needed to keep up your body weight and strength, keep body tissue healthy, and fight infections.

Eating habits for people with cancer can be different from the usual healthy eating guidelines. Healthy eating habits and good nutrition can help patients deal with the effects of cancer and its treatment.

What Should I Eat?

Patients often have many questions as to what they should or should not eat. Your nutrition evaluation will review a tailored care plan to meet your specific needs and goals, as well as assist with side effects of treatments and address dietary concerns.

Oncology Registered Dieticians can help improve side effects of cancer treatment including:

  • Weight Loss/Weight Gain
  • Nausea/Vomiting
  • GI Issues such as diarrhea or constipation
  • Taste Changes

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I meet with a benefits counselor? +

We recommend meeting with a benefits counselor as early as possible in your treatment journey, ideally shortly after your diagnosis or when your treatment plan is being developed. This allows time to explore all available options before treatment begins. However, our counselors are available to assist at any point during your care.

What information should I bring to my benefits counseling appointment? +

To make the most of your appointment, please bring your insurance card(s), information about your current coverage (including deductible and out-of-pocket maximum), a list of your current medications, information about your household income if you're interested in financial assistance programs, and any questions you have about the financial aspects of your treatment.

What if I don't have insurance or my insurance doesn't cover my treatment? +

Our benefits counselors can help you explore insurance options through Medicaid, NY Exchange Plans, or other programs. If you're uninsured or underinsured, we can also help identify pharmaceutical assistance programs, charitable foundations, and other resources that may help cover the cost of your treatment.

Is there a fee for meeting with a benefits counselor? +

No, benefits counseling is a complimentary service that we offer to all of our patients. We believe that helping you navigate the financial aspects of your care is an essential part of your overall treatment and support.

How do I schedule an appointment with a benefits counselor? +

You can request an appointment with a benefits counselor by speaking with your oncologist, nurse, or any member of your care team. You can also call our main number at (855) 528-7322 and ask to be connected with a benefits counselor.
