Recommended Screening Schedule by Cancer Type

These general guidelines may be adjusted based on your individual risk factors and health history. Our specialists will help determine the optimal screening plan for you.

Early Adulthood, Age 20-39

  • Breast: Clinical breast exam every 1-3 years, monthly self-exams
  • Cervical: Pap test every 3 years starting at age 21
  • Skin: Annual skin checks, monthly self-exams
  • Thyroid: Physical exam of the neck during regular checkups
  • HPV Vaccination: Recommended through age 26 if not previously received

Middle Adulthood, Age 40-49

  • Breast: Annual mammogram (begin at 40 for average risk), clinical breast exam annually
  • Cervical: Pap test every 3 years or Pap+HPV co-testing every 5 years
  • Colorectal: Begin screening at 45 for average risk (colonoscopy every 10 years or alternate method)
  • Prostate: Discuss PSA testing with your doctor (begin at 45 for high risk)
  • Skin: Annual skin checks by dermatologist, monthly self-exams
  • Lung: Annual LDCT for high-risk individuals (heavy smokers)

Later Adulthood, Age 50-64

  • Breast: Annual mammogram, consider 3D mammography
  • Cervical: Pap+HPV co-testing every 5 years
  • Colorectal: Colonoscopy every 10 years or stool-based testing annually
  • Prostate: Discuss PSA testing with your doctor annually
  • Skin: Annual skin checks by dermatologist
  • Lung: Annual LDCT for eligible high-risk individuals
  • Hepatitis C: One-time screening for those born between 1945-1965

Senior Years, Age 65+

  • Breast: Annual mammogram until age 75, then discuss with doctor
  • Cervical: Can discontinue at 65+ with adequate prior screening and no history of serious precancers
  • Colorectal: Continue until age 75, then discuss with doctor
  • Prostate: Discuss benefits/limitations with doctor based on health status
  • Skin: Annual skin checks by dermatologist
  • Lung: Annual LDCT for eligible high-risk individuals until age 80
