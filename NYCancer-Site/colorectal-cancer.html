Colorectal Cancer Treatment

Specialized, integrated care for colorectal cancer patients.

Colorectal cancer is the third most common cancer diagnosed in both men and women in the United States. At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we provide comprehensive care with advanced treatments and compassionate support through every step of your colorectal cancer journey.

Understanding Colorectal Cancer

Colorectal cancer starts in the colon or rectum when cells begin to grow out of control. Regular screening has dramatically improved early detection rates, leading to better outcomes. Our specialists work closely with you to develop a personalized treatment plan based on the specific characteristics of your cancer, its location, stage, your overall health, and your preferences.

Different types of colorectal cancer include:

  • Adenocarcinoma - The most common type, starting in the cells that make mucus to lubricate the colon and rectum
  • Carcinoid tumors - Slow-growing tumors that form in hormone-producing intestinal cells
  • Gastrointestinal stromal tumors (GISTs) - Rare tumors that can form anywhere in the digestive tract
  • Lymphomas - Cancers that start in immune system cells
  • Sarcomas - Rare cancers that start in blood vessels, muscle, or connective tissue in the colon or rectum

Colorectal Cancer Screening and Early Detection

Regular screening is essential for early detection. We offer comprehensive screening options including:

  • Colonoscopy - Examines the entire colon using a flexible tube with a camera
  • Flexible sigmoidoscopy - Examines the lower colon and rectum
  • CT colonography (virtual colonoscopy) - Uses CT scanning to visualize the colon
  • Fecal immunochemical test (FIT) - Detects hidden blood in the stool
  • Stool DNA test - Detects altered DNA from cancer or polyp cells

We follow the latest guidelines for colorectal cancer screening while personalizing recommendations based on your individual risk factors.

Treatment Options for Colorectal Cancer

Our multidisciplinary approach ensures you receive the most effective treatments. Your personalized treatment plan may include one or more of the following options:

Surgery

  • Polypectomy - Removes polyps during colonoscopy
  • Local excision - Removes superficial cancers and a small amount of nearby tissue
  • Colectomy - Removes part or all of the colon
  • Low anterior resection - Removes the upper part of the rectum
  • Abdominoperineal resection (APR) - Removes the rectum, anus, and part of the colon
  • Minimally invasive techniques - Laparoscopic and robotic-assisted surgery

Radiation Therapy

  • External beam radiation - Uses X-rays to target cancer cells
  • Endocavitary radiation - Delivers radiation directly to rectal tumors
  • Intraoperative radiation therapy - Delivers radiation during surgery

Medical Oncology

  • Chemotherapy - Uses drugs to kill cancer cells
  • Targeted therapy - Targets specific features of cancer cells
  • Immunotherapy - Boosts the body's immune system to fight cancer

Clinical Trials

We offer access to innovative clinical trials testing the latest treatments for colorectal cancer. Ask your doctor if you might be eligible for a clinical trial.
