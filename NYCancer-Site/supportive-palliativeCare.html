Understanding Supportive & Palliative Care

Supportive and Palliative Care is specialized medical care focused on providing relief from the symptoms and stress of serious illness. The goal is to improve quality of life for both the patient and their family. It is appropriate at any age and at any stage of a serious illness and can be provided alongside curative treatment.

Our team of board-certified palliative care experts ensures your quality of life throughout your treatment. They will discuss your symptoms, current treatments, and how your illness is affecting you and your family. Your palliative care expert will then develop a care plan based on your unique needs.

Our Supportive & Palliative Care Services

Comprehensive Symptom Management

Our team specializes in managing a wide range of symptoms that can occur during cancer treatment or as a result of the disease itself. We follow established symptom management pathways for common treatment-related issues, including:

  • Pain and discomfort
  • Chemotherapy-induced nausea and vomiting
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Shortness of breath
  • Loss of appetite and weight loss
  • Sleep disturbances
  • Anxiety and depression
  • Dehydration
  • Neutropenic fevers
  • Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) - blood clots in the leg
  • Pulmonary Embolism (PE) - blood clots in the lung

Emotional and Psychological Support

A cancer diagnosis and treatment can bring significant emotional challenges. Our supportive care team works closely with our psychological services to provide:

  • Assistance with coping strategies for anxiety and depression
  • Support for managing fear and uncertainty
  • Counseling to address grief and loss
  • Guidance for navigating changing roles and relationships
  • Help with spiritual or existential concerns

Goals of Care Discussions

Our team facilitates important conversations about your care preferences and priorities. These discussions help ensure that your treatment aligns with your personal goals and values. We will:

  • Help clarify your priorities and wishes
  • Discuss treatment options and their potential benefits and burdens
  • Assist with advance care planning and documentation
  • Support communication between you, your family, and your healthcare team
  • Revisit and adjust goals as your condition and circumstances change

Practical Support and Resource Navigation

We connect patients and families with resources to address practical concerns that can arise during illness:

  • Information about community support services
  • Guidance on navigating the healthcare system
  • Referrals to financial counseling and benefit programs
  • Assistance with transportation and housing needs
  • Connections to home care services when appropriate

Integrating Palliative Care with Your Cancer Treatment

Supportive and Palliative Care is not an alternative to your cancer treatment—it's an important additional layer of support that works alongside your oncology care.

When to Consider Palliative Care

Palliative care can be beneficial at any stage of illness, from the time of diagnosis through treatment and beyond. Consider asking about palliative care if you:

  • Experience distressing physical symptoms related to your cancer or its treatment
  • Feel overwhelmed by your diagnosis or treatment decisions
  • Need help communicating with your healthcare team about your care goals
  • Want support in maintaining the best possible quality of life during treatment
  • Experience emotional distress or practical challenges related to your illness

Coordinated Care Approach

Our palliative care experts work in close collaboration with your oncologist and the rest of your cancer care team. This coordinated approach ensures that:

  • Your symptom management plan complements your cancer treatment
  • All members of your healthcare team are informed about your care goals and preferences
  • Your medical care is aligned with your personal values and priorities
  • Communication between different healthcare providers is seamless and consistent

Common Misconceptions About Palliative Care

"Palliative care is only for end-of-life situations."

Fact: Palliative care is appropriate at any stage of a serious illness and can be provided alongside curative treatment. It focuses on improving quality of life from the time of diagnosis onward.

"Receiving palliative care means giving up hope."

Fact: Palliative care supports your treatment goals, whether they include cure, life extension, or comfort. It focuses on living well with your condition, whatever your prognosis.

"Palliative care hastens death."

Fact: Research shows that early palliative care can actually improve quality of life and, in some cases, may be associated with longer survival through better symptom management and support.

"Palliative care is only about pain management."

Fact: While pain management is important, palliative care addresses all symptoms and concerns, including emotional, social, practical, and spiritual needs.
