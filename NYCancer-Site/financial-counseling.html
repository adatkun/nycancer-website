Understanding Financial Toxicity

Financial toxicity refers to the financial burden and distress that patients may experience as a result of cancer care costs. This can include direct medical expenses, insurance deductibles and co-pays, as well as indirect costs such as loss of income, transportation expenses, and childcare needs. We recognize that financial concerns can significantly impact your quality of life and even influence treatment decisions.

Our Financial Counseling team is committed to helping you minimize financial toxicity through proactive planning, education about insurance coverage, and connection to assistance programs. By addressing these concerns early in your treatment journey, we aim to reduce stress and enable you to focus on your health.

Our Financial Counseling Services

Our comprehensive Financial Counseling program offers a range of services designed to provide support throughout your treatment journey. Our counselors work one-on-one with you and your family to address your specific financial situation and needs.

Insurance Navigation

Understanding your insurance coverage is crucial for managing treatment costs effectively. Our Financial Counselors provide:

  • Detailed explanation of your current insurance benefits, including coverage for cancer treatments
  • Review of your deductible, out-of-pocket maximum, co-insurance, and co-pays
  • Guidance on maximizing your current insurance benefits
  • Information about alternative insurance options if needed, including Medicare, Medicare Advantage, Medicare Supplemental Plans, Medicaid, and NY Exchange Plans
  • Assistance with prior authorizations and appeals for treatment coverage

Cost Estimation and Planning

To help you plan financially for your treatment, our counselors provide:

  • Estimated costs of your specific treatment plan
  • Explanation of billing processes and payment options
  • Development of payment plans when needed
  • Strategies for managing medical expenses alongside other financial obligations

Financial Assistance Programs

Our counselors are knowledgeable about various financial assistance programs and can help you:

  • Identify pharmaceutical assistance programs for medication costs
  • Apply for grants from non-profit organizations
  • Navigate the application process for financial assistance programs
  • Access resources for non-medical expenses such as transportation and living costs

Affordable Care Act (ACA) Guidance

Our Financial Counselors provide information and assistance regarding the Affordable Care Act, including:

  • Explanation of ACA protections for cancer patients
  • Guidance on selecting appropriate marketplace plans during open enrollment
  • Information about special enrollment periods for which you may qualify
  • Understanding subsidies and cost-sharing reductions that may be available to you

Our Team Approach to Financial Support

Financial support at New York Cancer & Blood Specialists involves collaboration among several specialized team members, each providing unique expertise to address different aspects of financial needs.

Financial Counselors

Our Financial Counselors focus on helping you understand and navigate insurance coverage, treatment costs, and payment options. They work directly with you to develop financial plans tailored to your specific situation and treatment needs.

Benefits Counselors

Our Benefits Counselors specialize in identifying and applying for assistance programs, grants, and pharmaceutical support. They help minimize out-of-pocket expenses through various external resources. For more information, visit our Benefits Counseling page.

Social Workers

Our licensed Social Workers can connect you with community resources for financial assistance with non-medical expenses such as transportation, housing, and utilities. They also provide emotional support and counseling to help manage the stress of financial concerns.

Prior Authorization Specialists

Our Prior Authorization team works directly with your insurance provider to secure coverage for prescribed medications and treatments, minimizing delays in care and unexpected costs.

Preparing for Your Financial Counseling Appointment

To make the most of your appointment with our Financial Counselors, please bring the following information:

Insurance Information

  • Insurance card(s) for all policies
  • Summary of benefits from your insurer
  • Any insurance correspondence about your cancer treatment

Medical Information

  • List of current medications
  • Treatment plan documents (if available)

Financial Information

  • Recent pay stubs or income verification
  • Previous year's tax return (for certain assistance programs)
  • List of monthly expenses related to medical care

Questions to Ask

  • Prepare a list of your financial concerns and questions
  • Note specific financial challenges you're experiencing

The New York Cancer Foundation

The New York Cancer Foundation is a 501(c)(3) organization that offers financial assistance grants for non-medical expenses and transportation to qualifying patients who are under care for a cancer diagnosis in New York state.

Financial Assistance Grants

The Foundation provides grants to help with expenses not covered by insurance, such as:

  • Rent or mortgage payments
  • Utility bills
  • Transportation costs
  • Childcare expenses
  • Other essential non-medical needs

How to Apply

Our Financial Counselors can help you determine if you qualify for assistance from the New York Cancer Foundation and guide you through the application process. For more information, visit www.nycancerfoundation.org or contact them directly at (833) 588-6923 or hope@nycancerfoundation.org.
