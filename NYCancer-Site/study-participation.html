Study Participation

Information about participating in clinical trials and what you can expect during the process.

Why Participate in Clinical Research?

Clinical trials are essential to developing new treatments for cancer and blood disorders. By participating in a clinical trial, you can:

  • Access new treatments before they are widely available
  • Receive expert care from leading specialists
  • Play an active role in your healthcare decisions
  • Help advance medical knowledge for future patients
  • Receive close monitoring of your health status

Your participation helps researchers answer important questions about the safety and effectiveness of new therapies, leading to better treatments for cancer patients worldwide.

The Clinical Trial Process

Understanding what happens during a clinical trial can help you make an informed decision about participation. Here's what you can expect:

1

Initial Screening

After expressing interest in a trial, you'll meet with our research team to discuss the study and determine your eligibility based on specific criteria. This may involve a review of your medical records, physical examination, and diagnostic tests.

2

Informed Consent

If you qualify for the trial, the research team will explain all aspects of the study in detail, including potential benefits, risks, required procedures, schedule of visits, and alternatives to participation. You'll receive a written informed consent document to review and sign if you decide to participate.

3

Baseline Assessment

Before starting treatment, you'll undergo a comprehensive evaluation to establish baseline measurements of your health status. This may include blood tests, imaging studies, and other assessments specific to your condition.

4

Treatment Phase

During the treatment phase, you'll receive the investigational therapy according to the study protocol. This may involve taking medication, receiving injections or infusions, or undergoing other procedures. The duration of treatment varies depending on the specific trial.

5

Monitoring and Follow-up

Throughout the trial, you'll have regular appointments with the research team to monitor your response to treatment, assess side effects, and conduct necessary tests. The frequency of these visits is determined by the study protocol.

6

Study Completion

After completing the treatment phase, you'll continue with follow-up visits to assess long-term outcomes. At the end of the study, the research team will discuss your options for ongoing care, which may include standard treatments or other clinical trials.

Patient Rights and Protections

Your safety and well-being are our top priorities. Here are the rights and protections you have as a clinical trial participant:

Voluntary Participation

Participation in any clinical trial is completely voluntary. You have the right to decline participation or withdraw from a study at any time without affecting your standard medical care.

Informed Consent

You must receive complete information about the study in understandable language, with opportunities to ask questions before deciding to participate.

Privacy Protection

Your personal information and medical records are kept confidential according to federal privacy regulations. Only authorized research personnel have access to your data.

Ethical Oversight

All our clinical trials are reviewed and approved by an Institutional Review Board (IRB), an independent committee that ensures the study is ethical and participants' rights are protected.

Ongoing Information

You will be informed of any new findings or changes to the study that may affect your willingness to continue participation.

Medical Care

You will receive appropriate medical care for any adverse events related to your participation in the clinical trial.

Costs and Insurance Coverage

Understanding the financial aspects of clinical trial participation is important:

Costs Often Covered by the Study

  • Investigational treatment or medication
  • Tests and procedures specifically required by the study
  • Additional monitoring related to the research
  • Research-related appointments with study doctors

Costs Typically Billed to Insurance

  • Standard care that would be given regardless of participation
  • Routine tests and office visits
  • Treatment for side effects or complications
  • Hospital stays required for standard care

Our financial counselors can help you understand the specific costs associated with a particular trial and work with your insurance company to determine coverage before you decide to participate. Many insurance plans cover routine costs of clinical trials, and some trials provide compensation for travel expenses or other participation-related costs.

Support for Trial Participants

We are committed to supporting you throughout your clinical trial experience:

  • Dedicated Research Team - Our experienced research staff is available to answer questions, address concerns, and provide guidance throughout your participation.
  • Coordinators as Patient Navigators - Clinical research coordinators serve as your navigators, helping you schedule appointments, understand procedures, and access resources.
  • Comprehensive Education - We provide educational materials and one-on-one discussions to ensure you fully understand your treatment plan and what to expect.
  • Multidisciplinary Support Services - Access to supportive care services including nutritional counseling, psychological support, and social work assistance.
  • Transportation Assistance - For some trials, transportation assistance may be available to help you attend study appointments.
