Breast cancer affects 1 in 8 women in the United States. At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we provide comprehensive care with state-of-the-art treatments and compassionate support through every step of your journey.

Understanding Breast Cancer

Breast cancer occurs when cells in the breast grow out of control. Early detection with mammography and prompt treatment have dramatically improved survival rates over the last few decades. Our specialists work closely with you to develop a personalized treatment plan based on the specific characteristics of your cancer, your overall health, and your preferences.

Different types of breast cancer include:

  • Ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS) - Cancer cells are found in the lining of a duct but have not spread outside the duct
  • Invasive ductal carcinoma - The most common type of breast cancer, which starts in the milk ducts and spreads to surrounding breast tissue
  • Invasive lobular carcinoma - Cancer that begins in the lobules (milk-producing glands) and spreads to surrounding tissue
  • Inflammatory breast cancer - A rare but aggressive form of breast cancer
  • Triple-negative breast cancer - Cancer cells that lack estrogen receptors, progesterone receptors, and HER2 proteins
  • HER2-positive breast cancer - Cancer cells with high levels of HER2 protein

Breast Cancer Screening and Early Detection

Regular screening is essential for early detection. We offer comprehensive screening options including:

  • 3D Mammography (Tomosynthesis) - Creates a three-dimensional picture of the breast using X-rays
  • Breast Ultrasound - Uses sound waves to create images of the breast tissue
  • Breast MRI - Uses magnetic fields to create detailed images of the breast
  • Clinical Breast Exams - Physical examination of the breast by a healthcare professional

We follow the latest guidelines for breast cancer screening while personalizing recommendations based on your individual risk factors.

Treatment Options for Breast Cancer

Our multidisciplinary approach ensures you receive the most effective treatments. Your personalized treatment plan may include one or more of the following options:

Surgery

  • Lumpectomy (breast-conserving surgery) - Removes the tumor and a small amount of surrounding tissue
  • Mastectomy - Removes the entire breast
  • Sentinel lymph node biopsy - Removes and examines the first lymph nodes where cancer cells are likely to spread
  • Axillary lymph node dissection - Removes multiple lymph nodes
  • Reconstruction options - Various techniques to rebuild the shape of the breast after mastectomy

Radiation Therapy

  • External beam radiation - Precise X-rays target cancer cells
  • Partial breast irradiation - Focuses radiation on the area where the tumor was removed
  • Intraoperative radiation therapy - Delivers radiation during surgery

Medical Oncology

  • Chemotherapy - Uses drugs to kill cancer cells
  • Hormone therapy - Blocks hormones that fuel hormone receptor-positive breast cancers
  • Targeted therapy - Targets specific features of cancer cells
  • Immunotherapy - Boosts the body's immune system to fight cancer

Clinical Trials

We offer access to innovative clinical trials testing the latest treatments for breast cancer. Ask your doctor if you might be eligible for a clinical trial.
