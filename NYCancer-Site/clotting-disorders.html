Blood Clotting Disorders Treatment

Expert care for thrombophilia, deep vein thrombosis, and other hypercoagulable conditions.

Blood clotting disorders, also known as hypercoagulable states or thrombophilia, are conditions that increase your risk of developing abnormal blood clots. At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, our experienced hematologists provide comprehensive care for patients with all types of clotting disorders, using advanced diagnostic techniques and personalized treatment approaches.

Understanding Blood Clotting Disorders

Blood clotting (coagulation) is a normal process that helps stop bleeding when you're injured. However, in blood clotting disorders, this process occurs inappropriately or excessively, forming clots that can block blood vessels and potentially cause serious health problems.

Blood clotting disorders can be inherited (genetic) or acquired (developing later in life due to other conditions or factors). Common types include:

  • Factor V Leiden - The most common inherited thrombophilia in the U.S.
  • Prothrombin gene mutation - A genetic variant that increases clotting risk
  • Protein C deficiency - Lack of a protein that helps regulate clotting
  • Protein S deficiency - Deficiency in another natural anticoagulant
  • Antithrombin deficiency - Reduced levels of a protein that inhibits clot formation
  • Antiphospholipid syndrome - An autoimmune disorder that targets phospholipid-binding proteins
  • Hyperhomocysteinemia - Elevated levels of the amino acid homocysteine
  • Acquired thrombophilia - Due to cancer, surgery, pregnancy, hormone therapy, or other factors

Clinical Manifestations and Diagnosis

Blood clotting disorders often manifest as:

  • Deep vein thrombosis (DVT) - Blood clots typically in the legs, causing pain, swelling, and redness
  • Pulmonary embolism (PE) - Blood clots that travel to the lungs, causing chest pain, shortness of breath, and potentially life-threatening complications
  • Cerebral venous thrombosis - Clots in the brain's venous sinuses
  • Portal vein thrombosis - Clots in the vein that carries blood from the intestines to the liver
  • Recurrent miscarriage - Some clotting disorders increase the risk of pregnancy loss
  • Arterial thrombosis - Less common in most thrombophilias, but can occur
  • Unusual clots - Clots in unusual locations or occurring without typical risk factors

Our advanced diagnostic approach includes:

  • Comprehensive medical history - Review of personal and family history of clots
  • Physical examination - To identify signs of clotting
  • Imaging studies - Ultrasound, CT scans, MRIs to detect existing clots
  • Blood tests - For specific clotting factors and genetic mutations
  • Genetic testing - To identify inherited thrombophilias
  • Specialized coagulation tests - To assess the blood's clotting ability

Treatment Options for Blood Clotting Disorders

Treatment for blood clotting disorders focuses on preventing clot formation, treating existing clots, and managing underlying conditions. Your personalized treatment plan may include:

Anticoagulant Medications

  • Heparin - Immediate-acting injectable anticoagulant for acute situations
  • Low molecular weight heparin (LMWH) - Injectable anticoagulant with more predictable dosing
  • Warfarin (Coumadin) - Oral anticoagulant requiring regular monitoring
  • Direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs) - Newer oral medications that don't require regular blood tests, including:
    • Apixaban (Eliquis)
    • Rivaroxaban (Xarelto)
    • Edoxaban (Savaysa)
    • Dabigatran (Pradaxa)

Antiplatelet Medications

  • Aspirin - For certain conditions, especially arterial clots
  • Clopidogrel (Plavix) - May be used in specific situations

Thrombolytic Therapy

  • Clot-dissolving medications - For severe or life-threatening clots
  • Catheter-directed thrombolysis - Targeted delivery of clot-dissolving drugs

Surgical Interventions

  • Thrombectomy - Surgical removal of a clot
  • Inferior vena cava (IVC) filter - Device to prevent clots from traveling to the lungs
  • Vascular procedures - To restore blood flow in affected vessels

Preventive Measures

  • Prophylactic anticoagulation - During high-risk periods (surgery, pregnancy, etc.)
  • Compression stockings - To prevent DVT
  • Lifestyle modifications - To reduce risk factors
