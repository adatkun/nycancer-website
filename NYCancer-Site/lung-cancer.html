Lung Cancer Treatment

Comprehensive, personalized care for lung cancer patients.

Lung cancer is one of the most common cancers in the United States, affecting both smokers and non-smokers. At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we offer cutting-edge treatment options delivered with compassionate care to support you through every step of your journey.

Understanding Lung Cancer

Lung cancer develops when cells in the lungs grow uncontrollably, forming tumors that interfere with the lung's ability to provide oxygen to the body. Early detection and personalized treatment approaches have significantly improved outcomes for many patients. Our specialists work with you to develop a customized treatment plan based on the specific characteristics of your cancer, your overall health, and your personal preferences.

Different types of lung cancer include:

  • Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) - The most common type, accounting for about 85% of lung cancers
  • Small cell lung cancer (SCLC) - A less common, typically faster-growing type of lung cancer
  • Lung carcinoid tumors - Rare, slow-growing tumors that rarely spread

Lung Cancer Screening and Early Detection

Regular screening is crucial for those at high risk of developing lung cancer. We offer comprehensive screening options including:

  • Low-Dose CT Scan (LDCT) - Uses a small amount of radiation to create detailed images of your lungs
  • Chest X-rays - A quick imaging test that can detect abnormalities in the lungs
  • Sputum Cytology - Examines mucus from the lungs for abnormal cells

We follow the latest guidelines for lung cancer screening while personalizing recommendations based on your individual risk factors.

Treatment Options for Lung Cancer

Our multidisciplinary approach ensures you receive the most effective treatments. Your personalized treatment plan may include one or more of the following options:

Radiation Therapy

  • External beam radiation - Precise X-rays target cancer cells
  • Stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT) - Delivers precise, high-dose radiation
  • Brachytherapy - Places radioactive material directly into or near the tumor

Medical Oncology

  • Chemotherapy - Uses drugs to kill cancer cells
  • Targeted therapy - Attacks specific genetic mutations within cancer cells
  • Immunotherapy - Boosts the body's immune system to fight cancer
  • Combination therapies - Uses multiple treatment approaches for better outcomes

Clinical Trials

We offer access to innovative clinical trials testing the latest treatments for lung cancer. Ask your doctor if you might be eligible for a clinical trial.
