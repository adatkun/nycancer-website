Psychology Services

Specialized mental health support for patients and families throughout the cancer journey.

At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we recognize that a cancer diagnosis affects not only your physical health but also your emotional and psychological well-being. Our Psychology Services are designed to address the mental health challenges that can arise during cancer treatment and recovery, providing essential support for both patients and their families.

The Importance of Psychological Support in Cancer Care

Cancer can bring significant emotional and psychological challenges that impact your quality of life and even influence treatment outcomes. Many patients experience anxiety, depression, fear, or difficulties adjusting to the changes that cancer brings to their lives and relationships.

At NYCBS, we believe that addressing these psychological aspects is a fundamental part of comprehensive cancer care. Our approach integrates mental health support with your medical treatment, recognizing that emotional well-being is essential for optimal healing and recovery.

Our Collaborative Approach to Mental Health

We follow a collaborative model that works closely with your medical team, ensuring a coordinated approach to your care. Currently, we are partnered with Forge Health for evidence-based mental health care for patients and families.

Common Cancer-Related Psychological Concerns

Our psychologists are trained to address a wide range of emotional and psychological challenges that can arise during cancer diagnosis, treatment, and survivorship.

Treatment-Related Anxiety

Anxiety about seeing your doctor, having treatment, scans, or tests is a common experience. Our psychologists can provide strategies to manage this anxiety and make medical experiences less distressing.

Depression & Hopelessness

Feeling down, upset about yourself or your appearance, or experiencing a sense of hopelessness about the future are natural responses to a cancer diagnosis. We provide support and evidence-based interventions to address these feelings.

Family & Relationship Challenges

Cancer can strain relationships with family and caregivers. We help patients and their loved ones navigate changing roles, communication difficulties, and anger or irritability that may arise.

Social Isolation

Some patients experience difficulty getting out of the house due to fears of meeting people, leading to feelings of isolation and loneliness. Our psychologists help address these fears and rebuild social connections.

Body Image & Intimacy Concerns

Changes to your body from cancer or its treatment can affect how you feel about yourself. We provide support for body image issues and sexual problems that may arise during or after treatment.

Fear of Recurrence

Worry that cancer will return after completing treatment is extremely common. We offer techniques to manage these fears and reduce their impact on your quality of life.

Sleep Disturbances

Difficulty sleeping due to anxious thoughts or images can interfere with recovery and quality of life. Our psychologists offer evidence-based approaches to improve sleep quality.

Survivorship Adjustment

Transitioning to life after cancer treatment can bring unexpected challenges, including dissatisfaction with your job, relationships, or uncertainty about the future. We help you navigate this important transition.

Our Psychological Services

Individual Therapy

One-on-one sessions with a psychologist provide a safe, confidential space to address your specific concerns and develop personalized coping strategies. These sessions can help you:

  • Develop effective coping skills for managing anxiety and depression
  • Process difficult emotions related to your diagnosis and treatment
  • Address specific challenges such as insomnia or body image concerns
  • Prepare for important medical procedures or treatment decisions
  • Navigate life transitions during and after cancer treatment

Family and Caregiver Support

Cancer affects not only the patient but also those who care for them. We offer support for family members and caregivers through:

  • Education about the psychological impact of cancer
  • Guidance on supporting a loved one with cancer
  • Strategies for managing caregiver stress and preventing burnout
  • Assistance with family communication and relationship challenges

Group Support

Connecting with others who share similar experiences can be incredibly powerful. Our group sessions provide:

  • Validation and normalization of your experiences
  • A sense of community and reduced isolation
  • Opportunities to learn from others' coping strategies
  • Ongoing support throughout different stages of the cancer journey

Psychological Assessment

Our team conducts comprehensive assessments to identify specific psychological needs and develop targeted intervention plans. These assessments can:

  • Identify symptoms of anxiety, depression, or other psychological concerns
  • Evaluate the impact of cancer on your quality of life
  • Assess cognitive functioning when there are concerns about "chemo brain" or other cognitive changes
  • Guide personalized treatment recommendations

Our Screening and Referral Process

Our team of social work case managers screens and identifies patients or caregivers who may benefit from additional behavioral health support. This integrated approach ensures that mental health services are accessible to those who need them most.

How the Process Works

  1. Initial Screening: As part of your comprehensive care, we conduct brief emotional health screenings during various points in your treatment.
  2. Needs Assessment: If concerns are identified, a more detailed assessment helps determine the most appropriate level of support.
  3. Referral: Based on your needs, we connect you with our affiliated mental health resources within the community, including our partnership with Forge Health.
  4. Coordinated Care: Your mental health provider works closely with your oncology team to ensure coordinated, comprehensive care.
  5. Ongoing Support: Mental health services are available throughout your cancer journey, from diagnosis through survivorship.

Mental Health and Cancer Survivorship

Psychological support remains important even after active treatment ends. Our survivorship care includes addressing the emotional and psychosocial effects of cancer that may continue beyond treatment.

Survivorship Mental Health Support

Our psychologists can help you navigate the unique challenges of cancer survivorship, including:

  • Adjusting to the "new normal" after treatment
  • Managing fear of cancer recurrence
  • Returning to work or previous activities
  • Addressing changes in relationships and social roles
  • Finding meaning and purpose in the post-cancer experience
  • Developing healthy lifestyle habits for ongoing well-being

When to Seek Psychological Support

It's important to know that seeking psychological support is a sign of strength, not weakness. Consider reaching out if you experience:

  • Persistent feelings of sadness, anxiety, or irritability
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Changes in sleep patterns or appetite
  • Overwhelming fear or worry
  • Withdrawal from activities or people you previously enjoyed
  • Thoughts of harming yourself or feeling that life isn't worth living
  • Difficulty managing treatment-related stress
  • Challenges in relationships with family or caregivers
  • Concerns about body image or sexuality

Many patients find that even a few sessions with a psychologist can provide significant relief and improved quality of life during cancer treatment and recovery.
