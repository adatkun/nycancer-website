Prostate Cancer Treatment

Advanced, personalized care for prostate cancer patients.

Prostate cancer is the most common cancer in men after skin cancer, with approximately 1 in 8 men diagnosed during their lifetime. At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we provide comprehensive care with state-of-the-art treatments and supportive services to guide you through every step of your treatment journey.

Understanding Prostate Cancer

Prostate cancer develops when cells in the prostate gland begin to grow uncontrollably. Many prostate cancers grow slowly and may not cause serious harm, while others are more aggressive. Our specialists work with you to develop a personalized treatment plan based on the specific characteristics of your cancer, your overall health, and your preferences.

Different risk categories of prostate cancer include:

  • Low-risk prostate cancer - Slow-growing cancer that may be monitored rather than treated immediately
  • Intermediate-risk prostate cancer - Cancer with moderate risk of progression
  • High-risk prostate cancer - More aggressive cancer requiring prompt treatment
  • Advanced or metastatic prostate cancer - Cancer that has spread beyond the prostate

Prostate Cancer Screening and Detection

Early detection is key to successful treatment. We offer comprehensive screening options including:

  • Prostate-Specific Antigen (PSA) Blood Test - Measures the level of PSA in the blood
  • Digital Rectal Exam (DRE) - Physical examination to check for abnormalities
  • Multiparametric MRI - Advanced imaging to visualize the prostate
  • Prostate Biopsy - Guided sampling of tissue to confirm diagnosis

We follow the latest guidelines for prostate cancer screening while personalizing recommendations based on your individual risk factors and preferences.

Treatment Options for Prostate Cancer

Our multidisciplinary approach ensures you receive the most effective treatments. Your personalized treatment plan may include one or more of the following options:

Active Surveillance

  • Careful monitoring with regular PSA tests and biopsies
  • Appropriate for low-risk, slow-growing cancers
  • Avoids or delays treatment side effects

Surgery

  • Robotic-assisted radical prostatectomy - Minimally invasive removal of the prostate
  • Open radical prostatectomy - Traditional surgical removal of the prostate
  • Nerve-sparing techniques - Preserves erectile function when possible
  • Pelvic lymph node dissection - Removes lymph nodes when necessary

Radiation Therapy

  • External beam radiation therapy (EBRT) - Precisely targeted radiation from outside the body
  • Intensity-modulated radiation therapy (IMRT) - Advanced form of EBRT
  • Stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT) - High-dose, precisely focused radiation
  • Brachytherapy - Placement of radioactive seeds directly into the prostate

Medical Therapies

  • Hormone therapy (Androgen Deprivation Therapy) - Reduces testosterone levels
  • Chemotherapy - Uses drugs to kill cancer cells throughout the body
  • Immunotherapy - Harnesses the immune system to fight cancer
  • Targeted therapy - Attacks specific features of cancer cells

Clinical Trials

We offer access to innovative clinical trials testing the latest treatments for prostate cancer. Ask your doctor if you might be eligible for a clinical trial.

We believe in treating the whole person, not just the disease. Our support services include:
