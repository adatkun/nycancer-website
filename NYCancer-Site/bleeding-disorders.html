Bleeding Disorders Treatment

Specialized care for hemophilia, von Willebrand disease, and other bleeding disorders.

Bleeding disorders are a group of conditions that cause abnormal bleeding or problems with blood clotting. At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we provide comprehensive care for patients with all types of bleeding disorders, offering advanced treatments and supportive care to help you manage your condition effectively.

Understanding Bleeding Disorders

Bleeding disorders occur when the blood cannot clot properly due to missing or defective clotting factors or platelets. These disorders can be inherited or acquired and range from mild to severe in their symptoms and impact on daily life.

Common types of bleeding disorders include:

  • Hemophilia A - Deficiency in clotting factor VIII
  • Hemophilia B - Deficiency in clotting factor IX
  • Von Willebrand Disease - The most common inherited bleeding disorder, caused by a deficiency or dysfunction of von Willebrand factor
  • Platelet Function Disorders - Problems with the platelets that help form blood clots
  • Rare Factor Deficiencies - Lack of factors I, II, V, VII, X, XI, or XIII
  • Acquired Bleeding Disorders - Develop later in life due to medications, other health conditions, or vitamin K deficiency

Symptoms and Diagnosis

The symptoms of bleeding disorders can vary depending on the specific condition and its severity but often include:

  • Easy bruising - Bruises that appear without apparent cause or from minor trauma
  • Prolonged bleeding - After injuries, surgery, dental procedures, or childbirth
  • Frequent nosebleeds - That are difficult to stop
  • Heavy menstrual bleeding - In women
  • Blood in urine or stool
  • Bleeding into joints - Causing pain and swelling, particularly in hemophilia
  • Bleeding into muscles - Causing pain, swelling, and potential nerve damage
  • Spontaneous bleeding - Without injury, especially in severe forms

Our team uses comprehensive diagnostic approaches to identify and characterize bleeding disorders:

  • Complete blood count (CBC) - Measures levels of red cells, white cells, and platelets
  • Prothrombin time (PT) - Tests how long it takes blood to clot
  • Partial thromboplastin time (PTT) - Another clotting time test
  • Platelet function tests - Assess how well platelets work
  • Factor assays - Measure the amount and function of specific clotting factors
  • Von Willebrand factor tests - Assess levels and function of von Willebrand factor
  • Genetic testing - To identify inherited bleeding disorders

Treatment Options for Bleeding Disorders

Our specialists develop personalized treatment plans based on your specific condition, its severity, and your individual needs. Treatment approaches may include:

Clotting Factor Replacement Therapy

  • Factor concentrates - Purified clotting factors to replace missing or defective factors
  • Recombinant factors - Laboratory-created clotting factors that don't come from human blood
  • On-demand therapy - Treatment given when bleeding occurs
  • Prophylactic therapy - Regular treatments to prevent bleeding episodes

Medications

  • Desmopressin (DDAVP) - Stimulates the release of stored factor VIII and von Willebrand factor
  • Antifibrinolytics - Help stabilize blood clots, particularly useful for mucosal bleeding
  • Hormonal therapies - For managing heavy menstrual bleeding in women
  • Non-factor replacement therapies - Newer treatments that work through alternative mechanisms

Advanced Treatments

  • Extended half-life products - Newer factor replacements that stay active in the body longer
  • Emicizumab - A bispecific antibody for hemophilia A that mimics the function of factor VIII
  • Gene therapy - Experimental approaches to correct the genetic cause of inherited bleeding disorders
