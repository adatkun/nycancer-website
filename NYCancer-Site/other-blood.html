Specialized care for platelet disorders, white blood cell disorders, and rare hematologic conditions.

Beyond anemia and common clotting disorders, there are many other conditions affecting the blood and bone marrow. At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, our experienced hematologists provide expert care for the full spectrum of blood disorders, from common to rare, using advanced diagnostic techniques and personalized treatment approaches.

Understanding Blood Disorders

Blood disorders can affect the three main components of blood—red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets—as well as the plasma and the bone marrow where blood cells are produced. These disorders can be inherited or acquired and range from mild to life-threatening.

Our specialists diagnose and treat a wide range of blood disorders, including:

  • Platelet Disorders

    • Immune thrombocytopenic purpura (ITP)
    • Thrombocytosis (high platelet count)
    • Essential thrombocythemia
    • Platelet function disorders
  • White Blood Cell Disorders
    • Neutropenia (low neutrophil count)
    • Leukocytosis (high white cell count)
    • Lymphocytosis
    • Eosinophilia
    • Histiocytosis
  • Bone Marrow Disorders
    • Myelodysplastic syndromes (MDS)

    • Polycythemia vera

  • Plasma Cell Disorders

    • Amyloidosis

Platelet Disorders

Platelets are small cell fragments that help form blood clots to stop bleeding. Disorders of platelets can result in either excessive bleeding or increased clotting.

Common Platelet Disorders

  • Immune Thrombocytopenic Purpura (ITP) - An autoimmune condition where the body attacks its own platelets
  • Thrombocytopenia - Low platelet count from various causes including medications, infections, or bone marrow disorders
  • Essential Thrombocythemia - A bone marrow disorder causing excessive platelet production
  • Bernard-Soulier Syndrome - An inherited platelet function disorder
  • Glanzmann Thrombasthenia - A rare inherited disorder affecting platelet function

Symptoms and Diagnosis

Symptoms of platelet disorders may include:

  • Easy bruising
  • Prolonged bleeding from cuts
  • Spontaneous nosebleeds
  • Bleeding gums
  • Blood in urine or stool
  • Heavy menstrual bleeding
  • Petechiae (small red or purple spots on the skin)

Our diagnostic approach includes:

  • Complete blood count with platelet count
  • Peripheral blood smear
  • Platelet function tests
  • Flow cytometry
  • Bone marrow examination when necessary
  • Genetic testing for inherited disorders

Treatment Approaches

Treatment depends on the specific disorder and may include:

  • Corticosteroids for ITP
  • Immunosuppressive medications
  • Thrombopoietin receptor agonists to stimulate platelet production
  • Intravenous immunoglobulin (IVIG)
  • Rituximab
  • Platelet transfusions for severe thrombocytopenia
  • Splenectomy for select cases of ITP
  • Cytoreductive therapy for essential thrombocythemia

White Blood Cell Disorders

White blood cells (leukocytes) are crucial components of the immune system. Disorders affecting white blood cells can compromise immune function or result in abnormal inflammation.

Common White Blood Cell Disorders

  • Neutropenia - Abnormally low levels of neutrophils, increasing infection risk
  • Chronic Granulomatous Disease - An inherited disorder affecting neutrophil function
  • Leukocytosis - Elevated white blood cell count, often due to infection or inflammation
  • Lymphocytosis - Increased lymphocyte count
  • Eosinophilia - Elevated eosinophil count, often related to allergies or parasitic infections
  • Langerhans Cell Histiocytosis - Overgrowth of a specific white blood cell type

Symptoms and Diagnosis

Symptoms vary widely depending on the specific disorder but may include:

  • Recurrent infections
  • Fevers
  • Fatigue
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Unexplained rashes
  • Weight loss
  • Night sweats

Diagnostic approaches include:

  • Complete blood count with differential
  • Flow cytometry
  • Bone marrow biopsy
  • Genetic testing
  • Immunologic testing
  • Imaging studies when appropriate

Treatment Approaches

Treatment is tailored to the specific disorder and may include:

  • G-CSF (granulocyte colony-stimulating factor) for neutropenia
  • Antibiotics for infection prevention or treatment
  • Immunosuppressive medications
  • Corticosteroids
  • Targeted therapies for specific conditions
  • Stem cell transplantation for severe inherited disorders
  • Treatment of underlying causes

Bone Marrow Disorders

The bone marrow is responsible for producing all blood cells. Disorders affecting the bone marrow can impact one or more blood cell lines and range from benign to malignant conditions.

Common Bone Marrow Disorders

  • Myelodysplastic Syndromes (MDS) - A group of disorders characterized by ineffective blood cell production
  • Myeloproliferative Neoplasms (MPNs) - Conditions causing overproduction of blood cells
  • Polycythemia Vera - Excessive production of red blood cells
  • Primary Myelofibrosis - Progressive scarring of the bone marrow
  • Aplastic Anemia - Failure of the bone marrow to produce sufficient blood cells

Symptoms and Diagnosis

Symptoms vary by condition but may include:

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Frequent infections
  • Easy bruising or bleeding
  • Shortness of breath
  • Enlarged spleen (splenomegaly)
  • Bone pain
  • Night sweats
  • Itching (especially after bathing)

Diagnostic approaches include:

  • Complete blood count
  • Bone marrow aspiration and biopsy
  • Cytogenetic analysis
  • Molecular genetic testing
  • Flow cytometry
  • Imaging studies

Treatment Approaches

Treatment is tailored to the specific disorder and may include:

  • Watchful waiting for mild conditions
  • Growth factors to stimulate blood cell production
  • Erythropoiesis-stimulating agents for anemia
  • Immunosuppressive therapy for aplastic anemia
  • Phlebotomy for polycythemia vera
  • JAK inhibitors for myelofibrosis and polycythemia vera
  • Chemotherapy for higher-risk MDS
  • Hypomethylating agents
  • Stem cell transplantation for eligible patients
