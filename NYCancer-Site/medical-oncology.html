What is Medical Oncology?

Medical oncology is a subspecialty of internal medicine that focuses on treating cancer with medications that travel through the bloodstream to reach cancer cells throughout the body. Medical oncologists are physicians who specialize in the diagnosis and treatment of cancer using these systemic therapies.

Our medical oncologists serve as your primary cancer physician, coordinating your overall care and working closely with other specialists to develop a comprehensive treatment plan tailored to your specific cancer type, stage, genetic profile, and individual needs.

Medical oncology treatments include:

  • Chemotherapy - Powerful medications that kill rapidly dividing cells
  • Immunotherapy - Treatments that harness the body's immune system to fight cancer
  • Targeted therapy - Drugs that target specific genetic changes in cancer cells
  • Hormone therapy - Treatments that block cancer-promoting hormones
  • Biological therapy - Substances derived from living organisms to treat cancer
  • Clinical trials - Access to innovative investigational treatments

The Medical Oncology Process

When you come to New York Cancer & Blood Specialists for medical oncology care, you can expect a comprehensive approach:

Initial Consultation and Diagnosis

  • Thorough evaluation of your medical history and symptoms
  • Comprehensive physical examination
  • Review of existing test results and imaging studies
  • Additional diagnostic testing as needed, which may include:
    • Blood tests
    • Imaging studies (CT scans, MRIs, PET scans)
    • Biopsies for tissue analysis
    • Genetic and molecular testing of tumor samples

Treatment Planning

  • Multidisciplinary tumor board discussions to bring multiple specialists' expertise to your case
  • Personalized treatment plan based on:
    • Cancer type and stage
    • Genetic and molecular characteristics of your cancer
    • Your overall health and medical history
    • Your preferences and goals
  • Consideration of clinical trials when appropriate
  • Comprehensive discussion of treatment options, potential benefits, and possible side effects

Treatment Delivery

  • State-of-the-art infusion centers with comfortable private and semi-private settings
  • Experienced oncology-certified nurses specializing in treatment administration
  • Convenient scheduling options including evening and weekend appointments at select locations
  • Advanced monitoring during treatment sessions
  • Integrated supportive care to manage side effects and enhance comfort

Ongoing Care and Follow-up

  • Regular follow-up appointments to monitor treatment response
  • Management of side effects and complications
  • Adjustments to treatment plans as needed
  • Survivorship care planning after treatment completion
  • Coordination with other specialists involved in your care
