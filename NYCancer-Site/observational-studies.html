Observational Studies

Advancing our understanding of cancer and blood disorders through non-interventional research.

Understanding Observational Studies

Observational studies are a vital type of research that helps us better understand cancer and blood disorders without directly intervening in patient care. Unlike interventional clinical trials that test new treatments, observational studies collect data on patients receiving standard care to answer important questions about disease patterns, outcomes, and quality of life.

These studies provide valuable insights into real-world experiences and long-term outcomes that complement the information gained from traditional clinical trials. By participating in observational research, patients contribute to medical knowledge while maintaining their current treatment plans.

Types of Observational Studies at NYCBS

Our research program conducts several types of observational studies:

Prospective Cohort Studies

These studies follow groups of patients over time to observe outcomes, identify risk factors, and understand disease progression. Participants provide information and samples at regular intervals, allowing researchers to track changes and associations as they occur.

Patient Registries

Registries collect standardized information about specific patient populations, creating valuable databases for research. These comprehensive datasets help identify patterns, evaluate real-world treatment effectiveness, and generate new research hypotheses.

Quality of Life Studies

These studies assess patients' experiences during and after treatment, focusing on physical, emotional, and social well-being. The insights gained help improve supportive care strategies and address the non-medical impacts of cancer.

Biomarker Studies

By collecting and analyzing biological samples (like blood or tissue), these studies identify markers that may predict treatment response, risk of recurrence, or other outcomes. This research is crucial for advancing precision medicine approaches.

Survivorship Studies

These studies focus on the long-term effects of cancer and its treatment, examining physical, psychological, and social factors that affect cancer survivors. The findings help develop interventions to improve survivors' health and quality of life.

Healthcare Utilization Studies

By analyzing patterns of care and resource utilization, these studies identify opportunities to improve healthcare delivery, reduce disparities, and enhance cost-effectiveness while maintaining or improving patient outcomes.

Current Observational Studies

Below are examples of observational studies currently open at NYCBS:

NYCBS Registry for Molecular Profiling in Advanced Cancer

Study Type: Patient Registry

Purpose: To create a comprehensive database of genomic alterations in advanced cancers and correlate them with treatment outcomes.

Participation Involves:

  • Consent to collect and analyze tumor genomic data
  • Permission to access medical records
  • Completion of questionnaires about treatment experience
  • Follow-up for up to 5 years

Eligible Participants: Patients with advanced solid tumors who are undergoing or have undergone molecular profiling.

Long-term Impact of Immunotherapy on Quality of Life

Study Type: Quality of Life Study

Purpose: To assess the physical, emotional, and social well-being of patients during and after immunotherapy treatment.

Participation Involves:

  • Completion of quality of life questionnaires
  • Optional interviews about treatment experience
  • Follow-up assessments for up to 2 years

Eligible Participants: Patients who are currently receiving or have completed immunotherapy within the past 6 months.

Learn More

Biomarkers of Response and Resistance to Targeted Therapies

Study Type: Biomarker Study

Purpose: To identify blood-based markers that predict response or resistance to targeted therapies in various cancer types.

Participation Involves:

  • Blood samples collected before treatment and at specific intervals
  • Permission to access treatment and outcome data
  • Follow-up for duration of treatment plus 6 months

Eligible Participants: Patients who are starting treatment with a targeted therapy for any type of cancer.

Learn More

NYCBS Survivorship and Late Effects Registry

Study Type: Survivorship Study

Purpose: To document long-term physical and psychosocial effects of cancer treatment and develop interventions to address them.

Participation Involves:

  • Annual health assessments and questionnaires
  • Permission to access medical records
  • Optional participation in intervention studies
  • Follow-up for up to 10 years

Eligible Participants: Patients who have completed cancer treatment and are in remission or have no evidence of disease.

Participating in Observational Research

Benefits of Participation

Participating in observational studies offers several benefits:

  • Contribution to Knowledge - Help researchers better understand cancer and blood disorders
  • Enhanced Monitoring - Some studies include additional assessments of your health status
  • Improved Future Care - Findings may lead to better treatments and supportive care for future patients
  • Opportunity to Share Your Experience - Provide valuable insights about the patient perspective
  • Minimal Impact on Treatment - Continue your standard care while participating

What to Expect

Participation in observational studies typically involves:

  • Informed Consent - Clear explanation of study procedures and requirements
  • Data Collection - May include medical record review, questionnaires, or interviews
  • Sample Collection - Some studies collect blood, tissue, or other biological samples
  • Follow-up - Regular check-ins to collect updated information
  • Privacy Protection - Strict measures to safeguard your personal information
  • No Treatment Changes - Your current treatment plan remains unchanged

Impact of Observational Research

Observational studies conducted at NYCBS have contributed to important advances in cancer care:

Identification of Risk Factors

Our observational research has helped identify factors that increase the risk of cancer recurrence or treatment complications, allowing for more personalized risk assessment and prevention strategies.

Improved Supportive Care

Data from our quality of life studies has informed the development of enhanced supportive care programs addressing physical, emotional, and practical challenges faced by cancer patients.

Real-World Treatment Effectiveness

Our registry data has provided valuable insights into how cancer treatments perform in diverse patient populations outside the controlled environment of clinical trials.

Biomarker Discovery

Samples and data collected through observational studies have led to the identification of new biomarkers that help predict treatment response and guide clinical decision-making.
