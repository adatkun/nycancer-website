Lung Cancer Screening

Early detection saves lives. Learn about our comprehensive lung cancer screening program.

Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer deaths in the United States. At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we're committed to changing that through early detection. Our lung cancer screening program helps identify lung cancer at its earliest, most treatable stages.

Understanding Lung Cancer Screening

Lung cancer screening is a process that checks your lungs while you have no symptoms. When caught early, lung cancer is more likely to be treatable and potentially curable. Our specialists use low-dose computed tomography (LDCT) scans to create detailed images of your lungs, capable of detecting even small abnormalities that might indicate cancer.

Regular screening is recommended for people who:

  • Are between 50 and 80 years old
  • Have a 20 pack-year smoking history (e.g., 1 pack per day for 20 years)
  • Currently smoke or have quit within the past 15 years
  • Are in generally good health

Even if you don't meet these criteria, you may still benefit from screening if you have other risk factors. Talk to your doctor about whether lung cancer screening is right for you.

Benefits of Early Detection

Finding lung cancer early through screening offers significant advantages:

  • More treatment options - Early-stage lung cancer can often be treated with less invasive procedures
  • Better outcomes - The 5-year survival rate for localized lung cancer is nearly 5 times higher than for lung cancer that has spread
  • Peace of mind - Regular screening can provide reassurance, especially for those at high risk
  • Detection of other conditions - Screening may incidentally find other lung problems that benefit from treatment

Studies have shown that lung cancer screening with LDCT can reduce lung cancer deaths by up to 20% in high-risk populations.

Our Screening Process

At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we've designed our lung cancer screening program to be thorough, convenient, and supportive:

Before Your Screening

  • Risk assessment - We'll evaluate your personal and family history to determine if screening is appropriate
  • Insurance verification - Most insurance plans, including Medicare, cover lung cancer screening for eligible individuals
  • Education - We'll explain what to expect during and after your screening

During Your Screening

  • Low-dose CT scan - This quick, painless procedure uses up to 90% less radiation than a conventional CT scan
  • Minimal preparation - No special diet or medication is needed beforehand
  • Brief appointment - The entire process typically takes less than 30 minutes

After Your Screening

  • Expert interpretation - Board-certified radiologists specialized in thoracic imaging will analyze your results
  • Prompt follow-up - You'll receive your results within 3 business days
  • Personalized recommendations - If an abnormality is found, our team will guide you through next steps
  • Smoking cessation support - We offer resources to help you quit smoking, which is the most effective way to reduce lung cancer risk
