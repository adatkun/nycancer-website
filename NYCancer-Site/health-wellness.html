Health & Wellness Program

A holistic approach to support your physical, mental, and nutritional health throughout your cancer journey.

At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we believe that comprehensive cancer care extends beyond medical treatment to encompass your overall well-being. Our Patient Health & Wellness Program is designed to provide you with tools and resources to improve your mental, nutritional, and physical health throughout all stages of your treatment journey.

A Holistic Approach to Cancer Care

Over time, our Health & Wellness Program has evolved into a vital component of each patient's treatment plan. By implementing a comprehensive approach that addresses multiple aspects of wellness, we ensure that your entire scope of care is being followed and supported.

We recognize that cancer treatment can affect many dimensions of your life, which is why we offer a range of supportive resources within our offices to complement your medical therapies. Our integrated approach helps maximize treatment outcomes while enhancing your quality of life during and after cancer care.

Behavioral Health Services

We recognize the importance of mental health as an integral part of your comprehensive cancer care. Our behavioral health services are designed to support you and your family through the emotional and psychological challenges that can accompany cancer diagnosis and treatment.

Comprehensive Mental Health Support

Our team of social work case managers assists with finding resources and addressing the emotional, psychological, and behavioral needs of patients and their families. We screen and identify patients or caregivers who may benefit from additional behavioral health support and connect them with our affiliated mental health resources within the community.

Collaborative Care Model

We follow a collaborative model that works closely with your medical team, ensuring a coordinated approach to your care. Currently, we are partnered with Forge Health for evidence-based mental health care for patients and families.

Common Cancer-Related Concerns

Our behavioral health professionals can help with many cancer-related challenges, including:

  • Anxiety about medical appointments, treatments, scans, or tests
  • Feelings of anger, irritability, depression, or hopelessness
  • Fear, isolation, or loneliness
  • Difficulty maintaining daily routines
  • Sleep difficulties due to anxious thoughts
  • Body image concerns
  • Sexual problems
  • Fear of cancer recurrence
  • Adjustment difficulties after cancer treatment
  • Ongoing support for patients on continued cancer therapy

Nutritional Counseling

We believe in a multidisciplinary and holistic approach to cancer treatment, which includes proper nutritional counseling. Good nutrition can help you maintain strength, prevent body tissue breakdown, and fight infection during treatment.

On-Site Nutritional Support

For your benefit, we offer on-site nutritional counseling while you are on treatment. Our registered dietitians will speak with you at various points during your therapy to ensure we are providing all possible support for your nutritional needs.

Personalized Nutrition Plans

Eating habits for people with cancer can differ from standard healthy eating guidelines. Our dietitians provide tailored care plans to meet your specific needs and goals, as well as assist with managing treatment side effects and addressing dietary concerns.

Managing Treatment Side Effects

Our oncology registered dietitians can help improve various side effects of cancer treatment, including:

  • Weight loss or weight gain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea or constipation
  • Taste changes
  • Decreased appetite
  • Difficulty swallowing

Palliative & Supportive Care

Our palliative and supportive care services focus on improving your quality of life throughout treatment by managing symptoms and addressing the physical, emotional, and spiritual challenges of living with cancer.

Expert Symptom Management

We have a team of board-certified palliative care experts who ensure your quality of life throughout your treatment. They will discuss your symptoms, current treatments, and how your illness is affecting you and your family.

Personalized Care Planning

Your palliative care expert will develop a care plan based on your unique needs, working alongside your oncology team to provide comprehensive support that complements your cancer treatment.

Supportive Care Throughout Your Journey

Palliative care is available at any stage of illness and can be provided alongside curative treatment. Our team helps with:

  • Pain management
  • Control of other physical symptoms
  • Emotional support for you and your loved ones
  • Assistance with treatment decisions
  • Coordination with your entire healthcare team

Chronic Care Management

Our Chronic Care Management (CCM) program is designed for patients with two or more chronic health conditions. This program improves the coordination of your healthcare and creates a team focused on your unique needs.

Patient-Centered Care Plans

The CCM program develops comprehensive care plans based on physical, mental, cognitive, psychosocial, functional, and environmental assessments. This holistic approach ensures all aspects of your health are addressed.

Enhanced Access to Care

CCM ensures you have plentiful access to care, with opportunities for you and your caregivers to communicate with your practitioners. The program also manages patient care services through systematic assessment of your medical, functional, and psychosocial needs.

For more detailed information about our Chronic Care Management program, please visit our Chronic Care Management page.

Social Work Services

Our licensed Master Social Workers provide essential support by assisting with case management, resource allocation, and coordination of care, helping you navigate the practical challenges that can arise during cancer treatment.

Comprehensive Support Services

Our Social Workers can assist with a wide range of patient-related services, including:

  • Transportation issues and limitations
  • Financial assistance through grants and scholarships
  • Referrals to community agencies and support services
  • Emotional support and brief counseling
  • Home care referrals
  • Housing situation concerns
  • Connection to support groups for patients and families
  • Palliative care and hospice education

Coordinated Referrals

Our Social Workers work closely with other members of your care team and can provide referrals to other specialized services, including Benefits Counselors, Psychologists, Nutritionists, and Chronic Care Management, ensuring you receive comprehensive support throughout your treatment journey.

Primary and Secondary Prevention

As part of our commitment to your long-term health, we provide education and resources for both primary and secondary cancer prevention.

Cancer Prevention Education

Our in-house dietitian and nutritional counselor work with patients at higher risk of relapse due to increased BMI and other obesity-related conditions. We implement cancer prevention education series focusing on prevention, screening, and early detection of other diseases for patients already diagnosed with one malignancy.

Symptom Management Pathways

We follow established symptom management pathways for common treatment-related issues, including:

  • Chemotherapy-induced nausea and vomiting
  • Dehydration and fatigue
  • Neutropenic fevers
  • Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) - blood clots in the leg
  • Pulmonary Embolism (PE) - blood clots in the lung

Survivorship Care

Survivorship programs are an important part of your follow-up cancer care. As more people live longer with cancer, we recognize the growing number of survivors who need specialized support.

Comprehensive Survivorship Planning

Survivorship care is designed to maximize your health and well-being after completing cancer treatment. This includes addressing the physical, emotional, social, and financial effects that may have begun at diagnosis but continue through treatment and beyond.

Wellness Recommendations

Your survivorship care plan includes wellness recommendations related to:

  • Exercise and physical activity
  • Nutrition and dietary modifications
  • Health promotion (smoking cessation, sunscreen use, alcohol moderation)
  • Complementary care options
  • Sexual well-being

For more information about our comprehensive survivorship program, please visit our Survivorship Program page.
