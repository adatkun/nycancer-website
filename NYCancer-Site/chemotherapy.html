What is Chemotherapy?

Chemotherapy is a type of cancer treatment that uses powerful medications to kill rapidly dividing cells throughout the body. While these drugs primarily target cancer cells, they can also affect some normal cells that divide quickly, which explains many of the side effects associated with treatment.

Modern chemotherapy has evolved significantly since its development in the mid-20th century. Today's chemotherapy treatments are more effective, precise, and accompanied by better supportive care to manage side effects. They can be used in various ways to treat cancer:

  • Curative therapy - To eliminate cancer completely
  • Neoadjuvant therapy - Given before surgery to shrink tumors
  • Adjuvant therapy - Given after surgery to kill remaining cancer cells
  • Palliative therapy - To reduce symptoms and improve quality of life
  • Maintenance therapy - To prevent or delay cancer recurrence

Types of Chemotherapy

At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we use various chemotherapy agents, often in combinations tailored to your specific cancer type and individual factors. Major classes include:

Alkylating Agents

These drugs damage DNA to prevent cancer cells from reproducing. They're commonly used for a wide range of cancers including:

  • Lymphomas and some leukemias
  • Multiple myeloma
  • Sarcoma
  • Lung, breast, and ovarian cancers

Examples include cyclophosphamide, cisplatin, carboplatin, and oxaliplatin.

Antimetabolites

These drugs interfere with DNA and RNA growth by substituting for the normal building blocks of RNA and DNA. They're particularly effective for:

  • Leukemias and lymphomas
  • Breast, ovarian, and intestinal tract cancers
  • Some other solid tumors

Examples include 5-fluorouracil (5-FU), capecitabine, gemcitabine, and methotrexate.

Anti-tumor Antibiotics

These medications, different from antibiotics used for infections, interfere with enzymes involved in DNA replication. They're used for various cancers, including:

  • Breast cancer
  • Lymphomas
  • Sarcomas

Examples include doxorubicin, epirubicin, and mitoxantrone.

Topoisomerase Inhibitors

These drugs interfere with enzymes called topoisomerases, which help separate the strands of DNA for replication. They're effective for treating:

  • Certain leukemias
  • Lung, ovarian, and gastrointestinal cancers
  • Some other cancer types

Examples include irinotecan, topotecan, and etoposide.

Mitotic Inhibitors

These medications, many derived from natural sources like plants, stop cells from dividing by interfering with microtubules. They're commonly used for:

  • Breast and lung cancers
  • Myelomas
  • Lymphomas

Examples include paclitaxel, docetaxel, vinblastine, and vincristine.

Targeted Therapy Agents

While distinct from traditional chemotherapy, many targeted agents are used alongside or as alternatives to conventional chemotherapy. These medications specifically target genetic features of cancer cells with often fewer side effects.

Identifying the Right Chemotherapy Approach

Our oncologists determine the most appropriate chemotherapy regimen based on multiple factors:

  • Cancer type and subtype - Different cancers respond to different medications
  • Cancer stage - Early vs. advanced disease may require different approaches
  • Genetic and molecular features of your cancer
  • Your overall health and ability to tolerate treatment
  • Previous cancer treatments you may have received
  • Treatment goals - whether cure, control, or symptom relief

At New York Cancer & Blood Specialists, we perform comprehensive diagnostic testing, including genetic and molecular analysis when appropriate, to select the most effective chemotherapy regimen for your specific situation.

The Chemotherapy Treatment Process

When you come to New York Cancer & Blood Specialists for chemotherapy, you can expect a thorough, personalized approach to your care:

Evaluation and Preparation

  • Comprehensive assessment of your cancer and overall health
  • Baseline testing including blood work, heart function tests, and organ function assessment
  • Port placement if necessary for easier medication delivery
  • Detailed treatment plan development, including medication selection and scheduling
  • Education about what to expect and how to manage potential side effects
  • Discussion of fertility preservation options when applicable

Treatment Administration

  • State-of-the-art infusion centers designed for comfort and safety
  • Various administration methods including:
    • Intravenous (IV) infusion - most common method
    • Oral chemotherapy - pills or capsules taken at home
    • Injection - into muscle, under the skin, or into a body cavity
    • Regional delivery - directly to specific areas of the body
  • Treatment schedules typically organized in cycles with treatment periods followed by rest periods
  • Specialized oncology-certified nurses administering your treatment
  • Premedications to minimize side effects when needed
  • Monitoring during treatment sessions

Ongoing Monitoring

  • Regular blood tests to check blood cell counts and organ function
  • Imaging studies to assess treatment response
  • Dose adjustments if needed based on response and side effects
  • Side effect management with medications and supportive care
  • Nutritional support to maintain strength during treatment

After Treatment Completion

  • Follow-up appointments to monitor for cancer recurrence
  • Surveillance imaging on a scheduled basis
  • Management of any long-term side effects
  • Survivorship care planning
  • Transition to maintenance therapy when appropriate

Managing Chemotherapy Side Effects

Chemotherapy affects cancer cells but can also affect healthy cells that divide rapidly. This can cause side effects, which vary depending on the specific drugs, dosage, duration of treatment, and individual factors. Modern supportive care has significantly improved our ability to prevent, reduce, and manage these side effects.

Common Side Effects and Management Strategies

  • Fatigue
    • Energy conservation strategies
    • Light exercise as tolerated
    • Proper nutrition and hydration
    • Medications if appropriate
  • Nausea and Vomiting
    • Preventive anti-nausea medications
    • Multiple medication options for breakthrough symptoms
    • Dietary modifications
    • Hydration support
  • Low Blood Counts
    • Growth factor injections to boost white blood cell production
    • Medications to stimulate red blood cell production
    • Blood transfusions when necessary
    • Infection prevention strategies
  • Hair Loss
    • Scalp cooling options for certain chemotherapies
    • Resources for wigs, scarves, and head coverings
    • Support for the emotional impact
  • Neuropathy (nerve damage causing numbness or tingling)
    • Dose adjustments when symptoms appear
    • Medications to manage symptoms
    • Physical therapy approaches
    • Safety precautions to prevent injury
  • Mouth Sores
    • Special mouth rinses and oral care regimens
    • Pain management
    • Dietary modifications

Advances in Chemotherapy

Chemotherapy has evolved significantly in recent years, with important advances that improve effectiveness while reducing side effects:

Precision Dosing

  • Pharmacogenomic testing to identify how you'll metabolize certain drugs
  • Personalized dosing based on individual factors
  • Adaptive dosing strategies that adjust based on your response

Improved Delivery Methods

  • Liposomal formulations that protect healthy tissues and target cancer cells
  • Nanoparticle delivery systems for better drug distribution
  • Antibody-drug conjugates that deliver chemotherapy directly to cancer cells
  • Regional delivery techniques that concentrate treatment at the tumor site

Combination Approaches

  • Carefully selected drug combinations that work synergistically
  • Integration with immunotherapy to enhance effectiveness
  • Sequencing with targeted therapies for optimal results
  • Metronomic chemotherapy (lower doses given more frequently)

Enhanced Supportive Care

  • Better anti-nausea medications and protocols
  • Advanced growth factors to protect bone marrow
  • Scalp cooling technologies to reduce hair loss
  • Improved nutritional support during treatment
